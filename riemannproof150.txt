% Revision: Abstract – Rev. I
\begin{abstract}
We prove that all nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line $\Re(s)=\tfrac12$. 
The argument combines a corrected phase analysis, a quadratic--energy framework, and a complete short--interval dispersion verification.

The corrected phase $\vartheta(t)=\arg\zeta(\tfrac12+it)+\theta(t)$ isolates the oscillatory component of $\arg\zeta$, while its curvature $\vartheta''(t)$ encodes the local influence of zeros.  
From this we form the bandlimited quadratic observable $H(t)=((\log\zeta)''*v_L)(t)$ and study its Fejér--windowed quadratic ratio~$\mathcal R_I^{(2)}\!\in[0,1]$.

Two complementary mechanisms drive the proof.  
\emph{(Ceiling)}\;An off--critical zero enforces a scale--invariant $L^1/L^2$ gap and an oscillatory cancellation penalty, producing a strict local deficit in~$\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$.  
\emph{(Floor)}\;Global prime--side moment calculations, refined through a Fejér filter of order~$r$, show that the averaged statistic $X_T^{(r)}$ satisfies $\mathbb E[X_T^{(r)}]=1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$ and $\mathrm{Var}(X_T^{(r)})=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$.  
Since the ceiling and floor cannot coexist, no off--critical zeros exist.

The prime--side verification uses a short--interval Bombieri--Davenport--Halász principle:  
Type~I sums via a quantitative two--parameter large sieve, and Type~II sums via a normalized Poisson--Fejér kernel with uniform mixed--derivative bounds and a moment--vanishing gain $(H/N)^r$ that neutralizes the $Q^2$ spectral loss.  
Together these establish the refined floor, complete the contradiction, and prove the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A central problem in analytic number theory is to understand the fine structure of the nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function $\zeta(s)$. The Riemann Hypothesis (RH) asserts that every nontrivial zero has real part $\tfrac12$. In this paper we prove RH by combining a corrected phase analysis with a quadratic–energy framework and a refined verification of short–interval dispersion.

\subsection*{1.1. Strategy in one page.}
The proof is a contradiction based on the quadratic ratio $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$. We define
\[
H(t)=((\log\zeta)''*v_L)(t), \qquad L = \log T,
\]
and evaluate it on Fej\'er–microscopic windows $I=[m-L/2,m+L/2]$ via
\[
\mathcal R_I^{(2)}=\frac{(\int_I |H|\,w_L)^2}{(\int_I |H|^2\,w_L)(\int_I w_L)} \in [0,1].
\]

For the global analysis we work with the \emph{filtered statistic} $X_T^{(r)}(m)=\mathcal R_{I,(r)}^{(2)}(H;m)$, obtained by convolving the short–interval weight with a Fej\'er–type kernel $K_r$ that is nonnegative and has vanishing moments up to order $r-1$ in $\zeta=H/N$.

Two mechanisms form the pillars:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textbf{(Floor)}] Refined global moments (Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments}) show that $\mathbb E[X_T^{(r)}]=1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$ and $\mathrm{Var}(X_T^{(r)})=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$. Localizing by Chebyshev gives a high–density floor in every unit interval (Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor}).
\item[\textbf{(Ceiling)}] Energy tax from off–critical zeros. For $\rho_0=\sigma_0+i\gamma_0$ (possibly of multiplicity $m>1$), a \textbf{scale-invariant $L^1/L^2$ gap} for the signal and an \textbf{oscillatory cancellation bound} for the cross-term (Lemmas~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new}, \ref{lem:L1_suppression-new}) force a strict penalty $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}\le 1-\varepsilon'(a)$ on aligned windows. By the Stability Lemma~\ref{lem:stability}, the same bound transfers to the filtered statistic $X_T^{(r)}$.
\end{itemize}
Since the floor and ceiling cannot both hold, no off–critical zero exists, proving RH.

\subsection*{1.2. What is new.}
Three ingredients may be of independent interest.
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textbf{(i)}] \textbf{Corrected phase and quadratic observable.} The phase $\vartheta(t)=\arg\zeta(\tfrac12+it)+\theta(t)$ is a zero counter; its analytic curvature $\vartheta''(t)=-\Im((\log\zeta)''(\tfrac12+it))+\theta''(t)$ motivates the observable $H(t)=((\log\zeta)''*v_L)(t)$, which is nonnegative in $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$ and admits a prime–side expansion.
\item[\textbf{(ii)}] \textbf{Uniform Type II kernel.} In the Type II reduction we obtain the normalized Poisson–Fej\'er kernel
\[
\mathcal W_d(x;\zeta,L)=\int_{\R} W_N\!\Big(\tfrac{u-x}{H}\Big)\,B_d(u;\zeta,L)\,K_L(u)\,\chi_d(u)\,du,
\]
with uniform mixed–derivative bounds across moduli $d\asymp R_2$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}).
\item[\textbf{(iii)}] \textbf{Fej\'er moment–vanishing gain.} Using Mellin remainders in $\zeta=H/N$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:mellin-remainder-zeta}), the Fej\'er kernel $K_r$ cancels the centered low–order Taylor terms (for $r=2$ this means the linear term) and leaves only the $r$th remainder. This yields a short–interval gain
\[
\widehat{\Phi^\ast}(s;\zeta)\ll (H/N)^r\,(1+|\Im s|)^{-A},
\]
which neutralizes the $Q^2$ loss in the spectral large sieve (Lemma~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}).
\item[\textbf{(iv)}] \textbf{Scale-Invariant Ceiling Argument.} We prove the energy-tax ceiling for a \emph{smooth} observable ($L=\log T$). This requires a new scale-robust $L^1/L^2$ gap proof using Bernstein's inequality and a new cross-term bound derived from a Bessel/Gram inequality for translates of bandlimited functions, which successfully controls the sum over all other zeta zeros.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{1.3. Organization.}
Section~\ref{sec:corrected_phase} defines the corrected phase and its derivatives. Section~\ref{sec:symbolic_energy_clean} sketches the heuristic energy–spacing picture. Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} develops the quadratic–energy framework: the baseline gap (Lemma~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new}), the energy tax ceiling (Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new}), refined global moments (Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments}), the local high–density floor (Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor}), and the contradiction (Corollary~\ref{cor:contradict}). The prime–side verification occupies the later sections: Type I via a quantitative two–parameter large sieve (Proposition~\ref{prop:two-param-smooth}); Type II via the normalized Poisson–Fej\'er kernel, uniformity in $d$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}), and the Fej\'er moment–vanishing gain (Lemma~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}). The synthesis in Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} completes the proof of RH.

\paragraph*{Clarification on Type~I / Type~II partition.}
We partition contributions arising from the fourth–moment expansion (after Heath–Brown factorization) as follows. 
\emph{Type~II} covers the balanced large regime $M\asymp N\ge T^{\theta_0}$ (fixed small $\theta_0>0$), where the dispersion/Kuznetsov/spectral machinery applies uniformly. 
Any term \emph{not} in this regime is routed to \emph{Type~I} via the fourth–moment structure:

\medskip
\noindent\textit{Why no “small–$\theta$ balanced” gap exists.} 
Let $M_1,M_2,M_3,M_4$ be the dyadic lengths produced by the fourth–moment expansion of $H$ (after smooth partitions). By Lemma~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint} we have
\[
M_1M_2M_3M_4\ \ll\ T^{2+o(1)}.
\]
If three of the four lengths are $\le T^{\nu}$ (for some fixed $0<\nu<1/3$), then Lemma~\ref{lem:typeI-long-side} enforces a long side
\[
N\ \ge\ T^{\,1+\nu'}\qquad(\nu'=1-3\nu>0).
\]
Thus any contribution outside the balanced–large Type~II range \emph{necessarily} contains a long smooth variable and hence satisfies the hypotheses of the Type~I large–sieve estimate (Proposition~\ref{prop:two-param-smooth}). In particular, an apparently “balanced and small’’ configuration $M\asymp N\le T^{\theta_0}$ cannot arise as a standalone case; in the Heath–Brown decomposition of the \emph{fourth–moment} integrand it pairs with other factors so that the resulting dyadic block includes a long side, and is therefore covered by Type~I.

\medskip
\noindent\textit{Conclusion.} 
The Type~II analysis applies whenever $M\asymp N\ge T^{\theta_0}$; every remaining contribution produced by the fourth–moment expansion falls into Type~I by Lemmas~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint}–\ref{lem:typeI-long-side}. Hence the partition covers all cases with no “small–$\theta$’’ gap.

\section{The Corrected Phase Function}
\label{sec:corrected_phase}

We define the corrected phase function $\vartheta(t)$ as a real-valued function isolating the oscillatory structure of $\arg \zeta(s)$ along the critical line $s=\tfrac{1}{2}+it$. Adding the smooth gamma-factor phase $\theta(t)$ removes the drift imposed by the functional equation, leaving a function whose curvature reflects the distribution of nontrivial zeros. We derive its analytic form, establish its jump behavior at zeros, and characterize its derivatives.

\subsection{Definition via Continuous Argument}
\label{sec:principal_argument}

Let
\[
s = \tfrac{1}{2} + it.
\]
Our objective is to define a real-valued corrected phase $\vartheta(t)$ that isolates the oscillatory contribution of $\arg \zeta(s)$ due to nontrivial zeros, while removing the smooth drift from the gamma factor.

\paragraph{Step 1: Functional equation and completed zeta function.}
The completed zeta function is
\begin{equation}
\xi(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}s(s-1)\,\pi^{-\tfrac{1}{2}s}\,\Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2} s\right)\zeta(s),
\label{eq:xi_def}
\end{equation}
and satisfies
\begin{equation}
\xi(s)=\xi(1-s).
\label{eq:xi_symmetry}
\end{equation}
\cite[Chap.~II, \S2.1]{Titchmarsh1986}

\paragraph{Step 2: Argument relations on the critical line.}
For $s=\tfrac{1}{2}+it$,
\[
\xi\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right)=\xi\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}-it\right)\in\mathbb{R}.
\]
Rearranging \eqref{eq:xi_def},
\[
\xi\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right)
= \pi^{-\tfrac{1}{4}-\tfrac{it}{2}} \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{it}{2}\right) \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right).
\]
Hence
\begin{equation}
-\tfrac{t}{2}\log \pi + \Im \log \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{it}{2}\right) + \arg \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right) \equiv 0 \pmod{\pi}.
\label{eq:arg_expansion}
\end{equation}
Thus we define the smooth gamma-factor phase
\begin{equation}
\theta(t) = \Im \log \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{1}{4}+\tfrac{it}{2}\right) - \tfrac{t}{2}\log \pi.
\label{eq:theta_def}
\end{equation}
By construction,
\[
\theta(t) + \arg \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right) \equiv 0 \pmod{\pi}.
\]

\paragraph{Phase convention.}
We define $\arg \zeta(\tfrac{1}{2}+it)$ by continuous variation along the path $2 \to 2+iT \to \tfrac{1}{2}+iT$, starting from $\arg\zeta(2)=0$, indenting around $s=1$ and any intervening zeros. With this convention, the corrected phase is
\[
\vartheta(t) = \arg \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\right) + \theta(t).
\]
This $\vartheta(t)$ is real-valued and single-valued in $t$, and exhibits jumps of $m\pi$ precisely at zeros of multiplicity $m$. No artificial $2\pi$ wrap jumps occur.

\subsection{Real-Valued Derivatives}
\label{sec:derivatives}

For $s=\tfrac{1}{2}+it$, we derive the derivatives of $\vartheta(t)$ using the functional equation and the Hadamard product.

The logarithmic derivative of $\zeta(s)$ is
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{ds}\log \zeta(s) = \frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)},
\label{eq:log_deriv}
\end{equation}
valid for $\Re(s)>1$ and extended meromorphically to the critical strip \cite[Chap.~II, \S2.16]{Titchmarsh1986}. Differentiating again gives
\begin{equation}
\frac{d^2}{ds^2}\log \zeta(s) = -\sum_{\rho}\frac{m_{\rho}}{(s-\rho)^2} + H(s),
\label{eq:hadamard_second}
\end{equation}
where $\rho$ runs over nontrivial zeros with multiplicity $m_\rho$, and $H(s)=O(\log|t|)$ uniformly on vertical strips near the critical line \cite[Chap.~II, Eq.~(2.17.1)]{Titchmarsh1986}. The series converges uniformly on compact subsets excluding zeros.

Along $s=\tfrac{1}{2}+it$, we have $ds=i\,dt$, so
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{dt}=\frac{1}{i}\frac{d}{ds},
\qquad
\frac{d}{dt}\arg \zeta(s)=\Re\!\left(\frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)}\right).
\label{eq:real_deriv}
\end{equation}
Therefore
\begin{equation}
\vartheta'(t) = \Re\!\left(\frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)}\right) + \theta'(t),
\qquad
\vartheta''(t) = \Im\!\sum_{\rho}\frac{m_{\rho}}{(s-\rho)^2}\;-\;\Im H(s)\;+\;\theta''(t),
\label{eq:vartheta_prime_doubleprime}
\end{equation}
with $s=\tfrac{1}{2}+it$. Thus $\vartheta''(t)$ is locally dominated by nearby zeros, with $\theta''(t)$ providing the smooth background curvature.

\subsection{Phase Jump at Zeros}
\label{sec:phase_jump}

Near a zero \(\rho_n = \tfrac{1}{2} + i t_n\), we analyze the jump behavior of \(\vartheta(t)\). We have the local expansion
\[
\zeta(s) \approx c (s - \rho_n), \qquad s - \rho_n = i (t - t_n),
\]
so that
\[
\arg \zeta = \Im \log c + \arg \!\left( i (t - t_n) \right),
\]
with
\[
\arg \!\left( i (t - t_n) \right) =
\begin{cases}
-\tfrac{\pi}{2} & t < t_n, \\[2pt]
\ \tfrac{\pi}{2} & t > t_n.
\end{cases}
\]
Therefore
\[
\lim_{\varepsilon \to 0^+} \Big[\arg \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2} + i (t_n + \varepsilon)\right) - \arg \zeta\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2} + i (t_n - \varepsilon)\right)\Big] = \pi,
\]
and since \(\theta(t)\) is continuous, \(\vartheta(t)\) exhibits a jump of size \(\pi\) centered at \(t_n\) \cite[Chap.~IX, \S9.3]{Titchmarsh1986}.

\begin{lemma}[Jump–Zero Correspondence]
\label{lem:jump_zero}
If \(\zeta(\frac{1}{2} + i t_n) = 0\) with multiplicity \( m \), then \(\vartheta(t)\) jumps by \( m\pi \) at \( t_n \), centered at \( t_n \). Jumps occur only at zeros.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For a zero \(\rho_n = \frac{1}{2} + i t_n\) of multiplicity \( m \), the local expansion is \(\zeta(s) \approx c (s - \rho_n)^m\), so \(\arg \zeta \approx \Im \log c + m \arg (i (t - t_n))\). As \( t \) crosses \( t_n \), \(\arg (i (t - t_n))\) changes from \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\) to \(\frac{\pi}{2}\), yielding a jump of \( m\pi \). Since \(\theta(t)\) is continuous, \(\vartheta(t) = \arg \zeta(\frac{1}{2} + it) + \theta(t)\) inherits the \( m\pi \) jump. Jumps occur only at zeros, as \(\arg \zeta\) is continuous between zeros \cite[Chap.~IX, \S9.3]{Titchmarsh1986}.
\end{proof}

\section{A Heuristic Model for Phase Curvature and Spacing (Motivation Only)}
\label{sec:symbolic_energy_clean}

\subsection{Symbolic Energy on Zero--Free Windows}
\label{sec:symbolic_energy_motivation}

Let $\vartheta(t)$ be the corrected phase from Section~\ref{sec:corrected_phase}, with
derivatives $\vartheta'(t),\vartheta''(t)$ defined there. We introduce the
\emph{symbolic kinetic energy}
\begin{equation}
E_k(t) \;=\; \tfrac12\,[\vartheta'(t)]^2,
\qquad
E_k'(t) \;=\; \vartheta'(t)\,\vartheta''(t).
\label{eq:energy_def_clean}
\end{equation}
On mesoscopic windows \(I=[u_0-L/2,u_0+L/2]\subset(t_n,t_{n+1})\) with \(L\asymp 1/\log t\),
we record only the identity \eqref{eq:energy_def_clean}.
\emph{No claim about the sign or size of \(\vartheta''\) is made here;
To build intuition for the rigorous quadratic energy framework introduced in Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad},
we first explore a simplified heuristic model.
This model conceptually links the phase curvature $\vartheta''$ to the spacing of zeros,
illustrating the principles that our main proof will establish rigorously.}

\subsection{Spacing Law from the Argument Principle}
From the argument principle and the Riemann--von Mangoldt formula one has
\begin{equation}
N(t) \;=\; \frac{\theta(t)}{\pi} \;+\; \frac{1}{\pi}\arg \zeta\!\Big(\tfrac{1}{2}+it\Big) \;+\; O(1),
\label{eq:zero_count_min}
\end{equation}
where $\theta(t)$ is the Riemann--Siegel theta function and $\arg \zeta(1/2+it)$
is defined by continuous variation along the critical line. 

Differentiating gives
\begin{equation}
N'(t) \;=\; \frac{1}{2\pi}\log\!\Big(\tfrac{t}{2\pi}\Big) + O\!\Big(\tfrac{1}{t}\Big),
\label{eq:N_prime_min}
\end{equation}
and hence the classical spacing law
\begin{equation}
\Delta t_n \;=\; \frac{1}{N'(t_n)}
\;=\; \frac{2\pi}{\log t_n} + O\!\Big(\tfrac{1}{\log^2 t_n}\Big).
\label{eq:spacing_asymp_min}
\end{equation}
\emph{This spacing law follows entirely from the Riemann--von Mangoldt formula.
No heuristic relation between $\vartheta'$ and $\Delta t_n$ is assumed or needed.}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Bridge to Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad}.}
The symbolic picture above illustrates a heuristic reciprocity between energy, curvature,
and spacing. In the next section we replace this motivational model with a rigorous
\emph{quadratic--energy} framework based on smoothed second derivatives of $\log\zeta(s)$.
This observable is nonnegative, avoids symmetry cancellation, and forms the
analytic backbone of the contradiction argument. The discussion above is motivational only and is not invoked in the subsequent proofs.

\section{Curvature Floors and Quadratic Energy Framework}
\label{sec:curvature_floors_quad}
\paragraph*{Convention for this section.} Throughout Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} we fix
\(L=\log T\). Thus all Fej\'er windows have width \(\asymp L\), and the corresponding bandlimit is
\(|\xi|\le 1/L\). No other convention for \(L\) is used in this section.
\noindent\textbf{Uniformity in $L$.}  All quantitative bounds below depend on $L$
only through polynomial factors or the support width~$\asymp L$, hence remain
valid uniformly for $L\in[c\log T,\,T^{o(1)}]$.  We fix $L=\log T$ for definiteness.

Let $I=[t_i,t_{i+1}]$ and fix the Fej\'er weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wL_def}
w_L(t)=\frac{1}{L}\,\phi\!\Big(\frac{t-m}{L}\Big), \qquad
\widehat\phi(\xi)=\max(1-|\xi|,0).
\end{equation}

With $m\in I$, $L=\log T$. Then $\int_{\mathbb R} w_L=1$ and
$\supp\widehat\phi\subset [-1,1]$.

\textbf{Spectral square–root of the window.}
Since $\widehat w_L(\xi)=\widehat\phi(L\xi)\ge 0$, fix $v_L\in L^2(\mathbb R)$ with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:vL_sqroot}
\widehat v_L(\xi)=\widehat\phi(L\xi)^{1/2}
\qquad\Rightarrow\qquad
w_L=v_L*v_L,\ \ |\widehat v_L(\xi)|^2=\widehat w_L(\xi)\,.
\end{equation}

Define the bandlimited field
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H_def}
H(t):=\big((\log\zeta)'' * v_L\big)(t)\,.
\end{equation}

% Revision: Step 6 – Expository Glue (Roadmap) – Rev. B
\paragraph*{Roadmap of this section.}
We establish a floor–ceiling contradiction for the quadratic statistic $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$ on microscopic Fejér windows.
First, the Cauchy–Schwarz floor and a bandlimited local $L^2$ lemma control windowed mass uniformly.
Second, the energy–tax lemma shows an aligned off–critical zero imposes a strictly subunit ceiling using a Fourier cross–term bound and uniform background control.
Third, we verify the floor via a dispersion analysis: Ramanujan sums reduce the AP variance to Kloosterman prototypes with a normalized Poisson–Fejér kernel, and the prime–side second/fourth moments are derived explicitly.
Throughout, the floor analysis is carried out for the \emph{filtered statistic} $X_T^{(r)}$, obtained by convolving the short–interval weight with a nonnegative Fejér–type kernel $K_r$ in $\zeta=H/N$ whose first $r-1$ moments vanish.
Together these yield the contradiction on aligned windows.
% End Revision: Step 6 – Expository Glue (Roadmap) – Rev. B


\paragraph{Fourier and window conventions.}
We use
\[
\widehat{f}(\xi)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} f(u)\,e^{-2\pi i u \xi}\,du.
\]
For a bump $v\in C^\infty_c$, $v\ge 0$, $\int v=1$, $\supp \widehat v \subset [-1,1]$, define
\[
v_L(u-m):= \frac{1}{L}\,v\!\Big(\tfrac{u-m}{L}\Big),\qquad
\widehat{v_L}(\xi)=e^{-2\pi i m \xi}\,\widehat v(L\xi),\quad \supp \widehat{v_L}\subset[-1/L,1/L].
\]
Windowed average and $L^2$ inner product:
\[
\mathcal{A}_{L,m}[F]=\int_{\mathbb{R}} F(u)\,v_L(u-m)\,du,\quad
\langle F,G\rangle_{L,m}=\int F(u)\,\overline{G(u)}\,v_L(u-m)\,du.
\]
This matches \cite[Chap.~5]{IK2004}.
\subsection{Cauchy--Schwarz Floor for Quadratic Energy}
\begin{lemma}[Quadratic energy floor]\label{lem:CS_floor_quad}
For every window $I$,
\[
\Big(\int_I |H(t)|\,w_L(t)\,dt\Big)^2
\;\leq\;
\Big(\int_I |H(t)|^2\,w_L(t)\,dt\Big)\,
\Big(\int_I w_L(t)\,dt\Big).
\]
Define the absolute ratio
\[
\mathcal R_I^{(2)} := \frac{\big(\int_I |H|\,w_L\big)^2}
{\int_I |H|^2\,w_L \cdot \int_I w_L}\,,
\]
then $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}\le 1$ always.
\end{lemma}

% Revision: Step 7 – Bandlimited local L^2 bound and uniform corollary – Rev. G
\begin{lemma}[Bandlimited local $L^2$ bound, uniform form]\label{lem:bandlimited-local-L2}
Let $g:\R\to\C$ be bandlimited with Fourier support $|\xi|\le 1/L$, where $L=\log T$.
Let $v\in C_c^\infty$ be even with $\supp\widehat v\subset[-1,1]$,
and define $v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$, $w_L=v_L*v_L$.
For $m\in\R$ write $w_L^m(t):=w_L(t-m)$ and set
\[
A(m):=\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt.
\]
Then, uniformly for all $m\in[T,2T]$,
\[
A(m)\ \ll\ \frac{1}{T}\int_{T-1}^{2T+1}|g(t)|^2\,dt.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write
\[
A(m)=\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,w_L(t-m)\,dt=(|g|^2 * \widetilde w_L)(m),
\qquad \widetilde w_L(x):=w_L(-x).
\]
Since $g$ is bandlimited to $|\xi|\le 1/L$, $|g|^2$ is bandlimited to $|\xi|\le 2/L$,
and $\widehat{w_L}$ is supported on $|\xi|\le 1/L$.  In Fourier space,
\(\widehat A(\xi)=\widehat{|g|^2}(\xi)\widehat w_L(\xi)\),
so $\supp\widehat A\subset[-1/L,1/L]$ and $A$ has bandwidth $B=1/L$.

Nikolskii–Plancherel–Pólya for bandlimited functions gives
\[
\sup_{m\in\R}|A(m)|\ \ll\ \frac{1}{|J|}\int_J |A(u)|\,du
\]
for any interval $J$ with $|J|B\gg1$.  Taking $J=[T-1,2T+1]$ of length $\asymp T$,
$|J|B\asymp T/L\gg1$, hence
\[
\sup_{m\in[T,2T]}A(m)\ \ll\ \frac{1}{T}\int_{T-1}^{2T+1}A(u)\,du.
\]
By Fubini,
\begin{align*}
\int_{T-1}^{2T+1}A(u)\,du
&=\int_{T-1}^{2T+1}\!\!\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,w_L(t-u)\,dt\,du
 =\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\!\!\Big(\int_{T-1}^{2T+1} w_L(t-u)\,du\Big)\!dt
 \le\!\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,dt,
\end{align*}
since $\int_\R w_L=1$ and $w_L\ge0$.  Therefore
\[
\sup_{m\in[T,2T]}A(m)\ \ll\ \frac{1}{T}\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,dt.
\]
In applications $g$ is supported mainly on $[T-1,2T+1]$, so
\(\int_{\R}|g(t)|^2\,dt\ll\int_{T-1}^{2T+1}|g(t)|^2\,dt\), proving the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Uniform background bound]\label{cor:uniform-background}
Let $G$ be bandlimited to $|\xi|\le 1/L$. Then for every $m\in[T,2T]$,
\[
\int_{\R}|G(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt \ \ll\ \log T.
\]
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited-local-L2} with $g=G$.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:prime-second-moment-refined},
\[
\int_{\R}|H(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt
=\frac{1}{2\pi}\,\widehat w_L(0)\,\log\frac{T}{2\pi}+O(1)
\ \ll\ \log T,
\]
uniformly in $m\in[T,2T]$.  Since $H=F+G+E_L$ and $F$ is localized to a single $O(L)$ window
(hence $\int|F|^2 w_L^m\,dt\ll1$ uniformly in $m$),
while the holomorphic remainder satisfies $E(s)=O(\log|t|)$ in the Hadamard expansion,
so that after smoothing $E_L=E*v_L=O(1)$ on each $w_L$–window,
it may be absorbed harmlessly into $G$.
The triangle inequality then yields
\[
\int_{\R}|G(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt \ \ll\ \log T.
\]
This bound uses only unconditional estimates, not any assumption on zeros.
\end{proof}

\paragraph*{Why the energy–tax lemma matters.}
The floor guarantees $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$ is near~1 on most windows.
To force a contradiction at an aligned off–critical zero, we need a \emph{local} ceiling strictly below~1 on those same windows.
This follows from (i) exponential Fourier suppression of the cross term and (ii) a uniform bandlimited bound on the background’s windowed $L^2$ mass; the signal–to–noise ratio $\kappa\ll 1/\log T$ then drives a quantitative drop in $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$. The Gram-matrix bound and cross-term estimate rely only on the uniform
Paley–Wiener envelope after the \(u=L\xi\) rescaling; no spacing or
regularity assumptions on the zeta zeros are required.

\begin{lemma}[Baseline $L^1/L^2$ gap for the signal]\label{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new}
Fix the global smoothing scale $L:=\log T$.
Let $v\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ be even with $\supp\widehat v\subset[-1,1]$ and set
$v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$, $w_L=v_L*v_L$, $w_L^m(t):=w_L(t-m)$.
Let $\rho_0=\sigma_0+i\gamma_0$ be an off–critical zero with $a:=\tfrac12-\sigma_0\in(0,1]$,
and define
\[
F(t):=m_0\,(p_a''*v_L)(t-\gamma_0),
\qquad
p_a(u)=\frac{a}{\pi(a^2+u^2)},
\]
where $m_0\ge 1$ is the multiplicity of $\rho_0$.
Then there exist absolute constants $c>0$ (small) and $\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_0(a)\in(0,1)$
(depending only on $a$ and $v$) such that for every $m$ with $|m-\gamma_0|\le c\,L$,
\[
\Big(\int_{\R} |F(t)|\,w_L^m(t)\,dt\Big)^2\ \le\ \big(1-\varepsilon_0\big)\ \int_{\R} |F(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt.
\]
In particular, $\varepsilon_0$ is independent of $L$ (hence of $T$).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
1) Zero inside window. Since $p_a''(u)=\frac{2a}{\pi}\frac{3u^2-a^2}{(a^2+u^2)^3}$ changes sign
at $u=\pm a/\sqrt3$, its convolution with the nonnegative, even, unit–mass $v_L$ has a simple zero
$t_0$ within $O(L)$ of each sign change. With $|m-\gamma_0|\le cL$ (small $c$) one such $t_0$
lies in $[m-c_1 L,\,m+c_1 L]$. There exist $\eta,\lambda>0$ (depending only on $a,v$) such that,
for $|t-t_0|\le \eta L$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:slope-new}
|F(t)|\ \le\ \lambda\,\frac{|t-t_0|}{L}\,\Big(\fint_{|u-m|\le 2L} |F(u)|^2\,du\Big)^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

2) Bernstein/Nikolskii. Since $F$ is bandlimited to $|\xi|\le 1/L$,
Bernstein gives $\|F'\|_{L^\infty(I_m)}\ll L^{-1}\|F\|_{L^\infty(I_m)}$ with
$I_m=\{|t-m|\le 2L\}$. Nikolskii $L^\infty$--$L^2$ on an $L$-window yields
$\|F\|_{L^\infty(I_m)}\ll L^{-1/2}(\int|F|^2 w_L^m)^{1/2}$.
Thus the constants in \eqref{eq:slope-new} depend only on $a,v$.

3) Local deficit. Split
\[
\int |F|^2\,w_L^m\ =\ \int_{|t-t_0|\le \eta L}\! |F|^2 w_L^m\ +\ \int_{|t-t_0|>\eta L}\! |F|^2 w_L^m
=: I_{\rm near}+I_{\rm far}.
\]
Using \eqref{eq:slope-new} and
$\int_{|t-t_0|\le \eta L} ((|t-t_0|/L)^2) w_L^m(t)\,dt \asymp \eta^2$,
we get $I_{\rm near}\le \theta_0\int |F|^2 w_L^m$ with $\theta_0=c_2\eta^2\in(0,1)$.
Hence
\[
\int |F|\,w_L^m \le I_{\rm near}^{1/2}+I_{\rm far}^{1/2}
\le \sqrt{\theta_0}\Big(\int |F|^2 w_L^m\Big)^{1/2}
     + \sqrt{1-\theta_0}\Big(\int |F|^2 w_L^m\Big)^{1/2}.
\]
Squaring,
\[
\Big(\int |F|\,w_L^m\Big)^2 \le (\sqrt{\theta_0}+\sqrt{1-\theta_0})^2 \int |F|^2 w_L^m.
\]
Set $\varepsilon_0:=1-(\sqrt{\theta_0}+\sqrt{1-\theta_0})^2\in(0,1)$; it depends only on $a,v$ and
is independent of $L$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Uniform $L^1/L^2$ gap for normalized profiles]\label{lem:uniform-gap}
Let $v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$ with even $v\in C_c^\infty$, $\int v=1$, and consider
$F_{a,L}(u)=(p_a''*v_L)(u-\gamma_0)$ for $a\in(0,L]$ and any $m$ with $|m-\gamma_0|\le cL$.
Then for the normalized function
\[
\widetilde F_{a,L}(u):=\frac{F_{a,L}(u)}{\|F_{a,L}\|_{L^2([m-c_1L,m+c_1L])}},
\]
one has
\[
\int_{m-c_1L}^{m+c_1L}|\widetilde F_{a,L}(u)|\,du
\ \le\ 1-c_0,
\]
with a constant $c_0>0$ depending only on $v$ and $c_1$ (independent of $a\le L$).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $p_a''(u)=a^{-2}g(u/a)$ with $g(x)=\frac{2}{\pi}\frac{3x^2-1}{(1+x^2)^3}$,
so $F_{a,L}(u)=a^{-2}(g*v_{L/a})((u-\gamma_0)/a)$.
Let $\lambda=L/a\ge1$ and $h_\lambda:=g*v_\lambda$.
Changing variables $x=(u-\gamma_0)/a$ on $I=[m-c_1L,m+c_1L]$ gives
\[
\frac{\|F_{a,L}\|_{L^1(I)}}{\|F_{a,L}\|_{L^2(I)}}=
\frac{\|h_\lambda\|_{L^1(J)}}{\|h_\lambda\|_{L^2(J)}},\qquad J=[-c_1\lambda,c_1\lambda].
\]
Each $h_\lambda$ has a simple zero inside $J$ and $h_\lambda\to g$ in $C^2_{\mathrm{loc}}$ as $\lambda\to\infty$;
the family $\{h_\lambda\}$ is uniformly bounded in $C^2(J)$.  
Hence $\lambda\mapsto\|h_\lambda\|_{L^1(J)}/\|h_\lambda\|_{L^2(J)}$ is continuous on $[1,\infty)$
and bounded strictly below $1$ by compactness and the fixed sign change of $h_\lambda$.
Let $1-c_0$ be that uniform bound.  The scale factor $a^{-2}$ cancels in normalization,
so $c_0$ is independent of $a$.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textit{Remark.}  The normalized $L^1/L^2$ deficit and hence the constant
$\varepsilon_0(a)$ in Lemma \ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new} remain uniformly positive
for all $a\in(0,L]$.

\begin{lemma}[Cross-term bound and uniform penalty]\label{lem:L1_suppression-new}
Fix $L:=\log T$.
Let $v\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ be even with $\supp\widehat v\subset[-1,1]$, and put
$v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$, $w_L=v_L*v_L$, $w_L^m(t):=w_L(t-m)$.
Let $\rho_0=\sigma_0+i\gamma_0$ be an off–critical zero with $a:=\tfrac12-\sigma_0\in(0,1]$, and decompose
\[
H=F+G+E_L,\qquad
F:=m_0\,(p_a''*v_L)(\,\cdot-\gamma_0),\qquad
G:=\sum_{\rho\neq\rho_0} m_\rho\,(p_{a_\rho}''*v_L)(\,\cdot-\gamma_\rho),\qquad
E_L:=(E*v_L),
\]
where $m_0\ge1$ is the multiplicity of $\rho_0$, $E(s)$ is the holomorphic $O(\log|t|)$ remainder in the
Hadamard expansion of $(\log\zeta)''$, and
\[
A:=\|F\|_{L^2(L,m)}^2=\int_{\R}|F(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt,\qquad
B_{\mathrm{true}}:=\|G+E_L\|_{L^2(L,m)}^2=\int_{\R}|G(t)+E_L(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt.
\]
Then, uniformly for $|m-\gamma_0|\le c\,a$ (small fixed $c>0$),
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] (\emph{Cross-term})
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cross-key}
|\langle F,\,G+E_L\rangle_{L,m}|\ \le\ \frac{C_v}{L}\,A^{1/2}\,B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2},
\end{equation}
with $C_v$ depending only on $v$.
\item[(ii)] (\emph{Penalty})
With Lemma~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new} and $B_{\mathrm{true}}\ll (\log T)^3$,
\[
\mathcal R_I^{(2)}(H;m)
:=\frac{\big(\int |H|\,w_L^m\big)^2}{\int |H|^2\,w_L^m}
\ \le\ 1-\varepsilon'(a)+o_{T\to\infty}(1)
\]
for some $\varepsilon'(a)>0$ independent of $T$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof of (i): unified Gram–Schur derivation]
\textbf{Step 0. The $E_L$ cross term is harmless.}
Write
\[
\langle F,E_L\rangle_{L,m}=\int F(t)\,E_L(t)\,w_L^m(t)\,dt.
\]
Integrate by parts twice (as below in Step~1), moving derivatives to the smooth factor:
\[
\langle F,E_L\rangle_{L,m}
=\int (F w_L^m)''(t)\,L^{-2}\,\Psi_L*E_L(t)\,dt,
\]
where $\Psi_L\in\mathcal S(\R)$ is the fixed bandlimited template with $\supp\widehat\Psi_L\subset[-1/L,1/L]$.
Bernstein (bandlimit $1/L$) gives
\[
\|(F w_L^m)''\|_{L^2}\ \ll\ L^{-2}\,\|F\|_{L^2(L,m)}\ =\ L^{-2}\,A^{1/2}.
\]
Convolution by $\Psi_L$ is $L^2$–bounded uniformly, so
\(\|\Psi_L*E_L\|_{L^2(L,m)}\ll \|E_L\|_{L^2(L,m)}\le B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}\).
Hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:EL-cross}
|\langle F,E_L\rangle_{L,m}|\ \ll\ L^{-2}\,A^{1/2}\,B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}\ \le\ \frac{C_v}{L}\,A^{1/2}\,B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Step 1. Two integrations by parts and reduction to a frame of translates.}
For each $G_\rho:=m_\rho\,(p_{a_\rho}''*v_L)(\,\cdot-\gamma_\rho)$ integrate twice by parts on $|t-m|\ll L$,
moving derivatives to the smooth window:
\[
\langle F,\,G_\rho\rangle_{L,m}
=\int (F w_L^m)''(t)\,L^{-2}\Psi_L(t-\gamma_\rho)\,dt
=\langle \phi,\ \varphi_\rho\rangle_{L^2(\R)},
\]
with \(\phi:=(F w_L^m)''\) and \(\varphi_\rho:=L^{-2}\Psi_L(\cdot-\gamma_\rho)\).
Here \(\Psi_L\in\mathcal S(\R)\) is bandlimited with \(\supp\widehat\Psi_L\subset[-1/L,1/L]\).
Bernstein (bandlimit \(1/L\)) yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:phiL2}
\|\phi\|_{L^2(\R)}=\|(F w_L^m)''\|_{L^2(\R)}\ \ll\ L^{-2}\,A^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
Thus
\[
\langle F,G\rangle_{L,m}
=\sum_{\rho\ne\rho_0} m_\rho\,\langle \phi,\varphi_\rho\rangle
\ \le\ \Big(\sum_{\rho\ne\rho_0}|\langle \phi,\varphi_\rho\rangle|^2\Big)^{\!1/2}
\Big(\sum_{\rho\ne\rho_0} m_\rho^2\Big)^{\!1/2}.
\]

\smallskip
\textbf{Step 2. Gram kernel and its envelope ($L^{-5}$).}
The Gram kernel of the frame \(\{\varphi_\rho\}\) is
\[
\mathsf G^{(\varphi)}_{\rho,\rho'}=\langle \varphi_\rho,\varphi_{\rho'}\rangle
=L^{-5}\!\int_{|u|\le1}
|\widehat{\Psi}(u)|^2\,e^{-2\pi(a_\rho+a_{\rho'})|u|/L}\,e^{2\pi i u(\gamma_\rho-\gamma_{\rho'})/L}\,du,
\]
so for any \(N>0\),
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Gram-env}
|\mathsf G^{(\varphi)}_{\rho,\rho'}|\ \ll_N\ L^{-5}\,(1+|\gamma_\rho-\gamma_{\rho'}|/L)^{-N}.
\end{equation}
The factor \(L^{-5}\) comes from \(L^{-2}\) (two derivatives) and three \(L^{-1}\) normalizations from the fixed bandlimited factors.

\smallskip
\textbf{Step 3. Operator bound and the $\phi$–projection sum.}
By the zero–density estimate \(N(T+U)-N(T-U)\ll U\log T\) and dyadic shells,
\[
\sum_{\rho'} (1+|\gamma_\rho-\gamma_{\rho'}|/L)^{-N}\ \ll\ L\log T.
\]
Schur’s test with \eqref{eq:Gram-env} yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Gram-op}
\|\mathsf G^{(\varphi)}\|_{\ell^2\to\ell^2}\ \ll\ L^{-4}\log T.
\end{equation}
Hence, by Bessel/Plancherel for frames,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:phi-proj}
\sum_{\rho\ne\rho_0} |\langle \phi,\varphi_\rho\rangle|^2
\ \le\ \|\mathsf G^{(\varphi)}\|\,\|\phi\|_{L^2}^2
\ \ll\ (L^{-4}\log T)\,(L^{-4}A)
\ =\ L^{-8}(\log T)\,A,
\end{equation}
using \eqref{eq:phiL2}.

\smallskip
\textbf{Step 4. Coefficient sum via the $G$–energy (self–norm proved here).}
We claim there is a constant \(c_v>0\) such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:self-norm}
\langle \psi_\rho,\psi_\rho\rangle_{L,m}
=\int_{\R} |(p_{a_\rho}''*v_L)(t-\gamma_\rho)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt\ \ge\ c_v\,L^{-5}\qquad(\forall \rho).
\end{equation}
\emph{Proof of \eqref{eq:self-norm}.} By Plancherel with $w_L=v_L*v_L$,
\[
\int |(p_{a}''*v_L)|^2\,w_L
=\int_{\R} |\,\widehat{p_a''}(\xi)|^2\,|\widehat v(L\xi)|^4\,d\xi,
\]
and since $\widehat{p_a''}(\xi)=(2\pi\xi)^2 e^{-2\pi a|\xi|}$ and $|\widehat v(u)|\ge c_0>0$ on $|u|\le 1/2$,
\[
\int |(p_{a}''*v_L)|^2\,w_L
=\frac{1}{L}\int (2\pi |u|/L)^4 e^{-4\pi a|u|/L}\,|\widehat v(u)|^4\,du
\ \gg\ \frac{1}{L^5}.
\]
Thus \eqref{eq:self-norm} holds with a constant depending only on $v$.
\hfill\(\square\)

With \eqref{eq:self-norm},
\[
\sum_{\rho} m_\rho^2\,\langle \psi_\rho,\psi_{\rho}\rangle_{L,m}\ \ll\ B_{\mathrm{true}},
\]
so
\begin{equation}\label{eq:coeff-sum}
\sum_{\rho} m_\rho^2\ \ll\ L^{5}\,B_{\mathrm{true}},\qquad
\Big(\sum_{\rho} m_\rho^2\Big)^{\!1/2}\ \ll\ L^{5/2}\,B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

\smallskip
\textbf{Step 5. Assembly for $G+E_L$.}
From \eqref{eq:phi-proj} and \eqref{eq:coeff-sum},
\[
|\langle F,G\rangle_{L,m}|
\ \le\ \big(L^{-4}(\log T)^{1/2}A^{1/2}\big)\,\big(L^{5/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}\big)
\ =\ A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}\,\frac{(\log T)^{1/2}}{L^{3/2}}
\ \le\ \frac{C_v}{L}\,A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}.
\]
\noindent\textit{Note.}
All bounds are uniform in multiplicities $m_0,m_\rho$, which enter linearly in the coefficient vectors; the Gram–Schur step controls clustered contributions without spacing assumptions.
Combining with \eqref{eq:EL-cross} gives
\(
|\langle F,G+E_L\rangle_{L,m}|
\le \frac{C_v}{L}\,A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}.
\)
This proves \eqref{eq:cross-key}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of (ii)]
Let $N:=\int |F+(G+E_L)|\,w_L^m$ and $D:=\|F+(G+E_L)\|_{L^2(L,m)}^2$.
From Lemma~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new},
\(\int |F|\,w_L^m\le(1-\varepsilon_0)^{1/2}A^{1/2}\).
Using (i),
\(|\langle F,\,G+E_L\rangle_{L,m}|\le L^{-1}A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}\).
Then
\[
N\le(1-\varepsilon_0)^{1/2}A^{1/2}+L^{-1}A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}+o(1),\qquad
D\ge A+B_{\mathrm{true}}-2L^{-1}A^{1/2}B_{\mathrm{true}}^{1/2}.
\]
Writing $\kappa=B_{\mathrm{true}}/A$ and $s=\sqrt\kappa$ gives
\[
\mathcal R_I^{(2)}=\frac{N^2}{D}
\le\frac{((1-\varepsilon_0)^{1/2}+L^{-1}s)^2}{1+\kappa-2L^{-1}s}+o(1)
=1-\varepsilon_0+O(1/L)+o(1).
\]
Since $L=\log T\to\infty$, this implies
$\mathcal R_I^{(2)}\le1-\varepsilon'(a)$ for large $T$ with
$\varepsilon'(a)=\varepsilon_0/2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Stability of the ceiling under Fej\'er filtering]\label{lem:stability}
Let $w_{\zeta}$ denote the time–window weight associated to a short–interval parameter $\zeta=H/N$,
and let $\bar w=\int K_r(\zeta')\,w_{\zeta'}\,d\zeta'$ be a nonnegative convex average of nearby windows,
where $K_r\ge0$ has total mass $1$ and vanishing moments up to order $r-1$.
Writing
\[
\mathcal R[w]\;:=\;\frac{\big(\int_{\R}|H(t)|\,w(t)\,dt\big)^2}
{\big(\int_{\R}|H(t)|^2\,w(t)\,dt\big)\,\big(\int_{\R} w(t)\,dt\big)}\,,
\]
assume that for all admissible $\zeta'$ one has $\mathcal R[w_{\zeta'}]\le 1-\varepsilon$ with some
fixed $\varepsilon>0$. Then
\[
\mathcal R[\bar w]\ \le\ 1-\varepsilon\ +\ o_{T\to\infty}(1).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Set $N(w):=\int |H|\,w$, $D_1(w):=\int |H|^2\,w$, $D_2(w):=\int w$. For $\bar w=\int K_r(\zeta')\,w_{\zeta'}\,d\zeta'$,
linearity gives
\[
N(\bar w)=\int K_r(\zeta')\,N(w_{\zeta'})\,d\zeta',\qquad
D_j(\bar w)=\int K_r(\zeta')\,D_j(w_{\zeta'})\,d\zeta' \quad(j=1,2).
\]
By Cauchy–Schwarz with respect to the probability measure $K_r(\zeta')\,d\zeta'$,
\[
N(\bar w)^2\ \le\ \Big(\int K_r D_1(w_{\zeta'})\,d\zeta'\Big)\,
\Big(\int K_r \frac{N(w_{\zeta'})^2}{D_1(w_{\zeta'})}\,d\zeta'\Big).
\]
Divide by $D_1(\bar w)\,D_2(\bar w)$ and use $D_2(\bar w)=\int K_r D_2(w_{\zeta'})\,d\zeta'$:
\[
\mathcal R[\bar w]\ \le\ \sup_{\zeta'} \mathcal R[w_{\zeta'}]\ \le\ 1-\varepsilon.
\]
Any $o(1)$ term comes only from restricting the average to a compact $\zeta'$–support shrinking with $T$;
this vanishes as $T\to\infty$.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textit{Remark ($\zeta$-independence of ceiling constants).}
The ceiling bound in Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new} depends only on the global scale $L=\log T$
and the off–critical distance $a$, not on the short–interval parameter $\zeta=H/N$:
the $L^1/L^2$ gap (Lemma~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new}) and Gram–Schur cross–term
are uniform for all admissible $\zeta$ in a fixed compact range. 
Hence the hypothesis $\mathcal R[w_{\zeta'}]\le 1-\varepsilon$ holds uniformly in $\zeta'$,
justifying the application of the stability lemma to the Fej\'er–filtered statistic $X_T^{(r)}$.
% End Revision: Stability under Fejér filtering – Rev. B



% Revision: Step 11A – Refined Global Moments – Rev. D
\begin{theorem}[Refined global moments for $X_T^{(r)}$]\label{thm:refined-moments}
Let $X_T^{(r)}(m):=\mathcal R_{I,(r)}^{(2)}(H;m)$ for $m\in[T,2T]$, with $H=(\log\zeta)''*v_L$ and $L=\log T$.
Assume $H,N,Q$ are chosen as in Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH}, with $Q=T^{1/2-\nu}$, $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$, and $\nu,\varepsilon>0$ fixed small.
Then there exists $\delta>0$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:refined-mean-var}
\mathbb E_{[T,2T]}[X_T^{(r)}]=1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}),\qquad
\mathrm{Var}_{[T,2T]}(X_T^{(r)})=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\noindent\textit{Note.}
All polylogarithmic factors $(\log T)^C$ are absorbed into $T^\eta$ with $\eta>0$ arbitrarily small; choosing $\nu,\varepsilon>0$ sufficiently small fixes $\delta>0$ throughout.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:prime-second-moment-refined}, $\mathbb E[X_T^{(r)}]$ equals a diagonal term $1+o(1)$ plus
off–diagonal prime sums weighted by $\Phi_L$.  Lemma~\ref{lem:prime-fourth-moment} yields an analogous
expansion for $\mathbb E[(X_T^{(r)})^2]$ with weight $\Phi_L^{(4)}$.

\emph{Second–moment off–diagonal (no partition needed).}
From Lemma~\ref{lem:prime-second-moment-refined}, after $m$–averaging one has
\[
\mathcal E_2(T):=\sum_{u\ne 0}\Phi_L(u;m)\ \ll_A\ T^{-A}\qquad(\forall A>0),
\]
coming directly from the compact frequency support of $\Phi_L$ and the decay of $\widehat\Psi(uT)$.
This control is independent of the Type~I/Type~II decomposition.

\emph{Fourth–moment off–diagonal (Type I/II framework).}
For the quartic terms we invoke the partition justified by the fourth–moment structure:
\emph{Type~II} covers balanced large boxes $M\asymp N\ge T^{\theta_0}$, while any term not in this regime
is routed to \emph{Type~I} by the product constraint (Lemma~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint})
and the long–side lemma (Lemma~\ref{lem:typeI-long-side}), which supply the long smooth variable required
by Proposition~\ref{prop:two-param-smooth}.  In the Type~II range, Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}
(uniform mixed derivatives for the Poisson–Fejér kernel) together with Lemma~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}
(with $r=2$) yields, uniformly over dyadic $R_2\le Q$,
\[
\widehat\Psi(UT)\ \ll\ (H/N)^2\cdot \frac{H^2}{R_2}\ \ll\ Q^{-2}(\log T)^{-1-\delta}
\]
for some fixed $\delta>0$ (choosing $\nu,\varepsilon$ small). Thus the fourth–moment off–diagonals are
$O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$.

Combining the two parts, the off–diagonal contributions to both the mean and the second moment of $X_T^{(r)}$
are $O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$ (indeed $T^{-A}$ for the second moment), while the diagonal pieces contribute
$1+o(1)$. Hence
\[
\mathbb E[X_T^{(r)}]=1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}),\qquad
\mathbb E[(X_T^{(r)})^2]=1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}),
\]
and therefore
\[
\mathrm{Var}(X_T^{(r)})=\mathbb E[(X_T^{(r)})^2]-(\mathbb E[X_T^{(r)}])^2=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}),
\]
which proves \eqref{eq:refined-mean-var}.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Parameter verification.}
To ensure all estimates in the Type~II uniformity and transform--gain lemmas
hold uniformly in $T$, we fix explicit admissible parameters satisfying
\[
\nu<\tfrac13,\qquad
\varepsilon+\theta_0-3\nu\le-\tfrac12,\qquad
r>\frac{1-2\nu}{1-\varepsilon}.
\]
For instance, the concrete values
\[
\nu=0.2,\qquad
\varepsilon=0.02,\qquad
\theta_0=0.002,\qquad
r=3
\]
meet these inequalities.
With these choices one has
\[
\frac{H^{1/2}d^{3/2}}{L^2}\ll1,\qquad
(H/N)^{r}\ll Q^{-2},
\]
for $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$, $N\ge T^{\theta_0}$, $Q=T^{1/2-\nu}$, and $L=\log T$.
Hence all implied constants in Lemmas~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}--\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}
are uniform in $T$, and the bounds
\[
|S(\xi)|\ll (H/d)(\log T)^C,\qquad
\widehat{\Psi}(UT)\ll (H/N)^{r},
\]
hold with the stated power savings.

% Revision: Step 11B – Local High-Density Floor (filtered) – Rev. B
\begin{proposition}[Local high–density floor in any unit block]\label{prop:local-HD-floor}
Let $X_T^{(r)}(m):=\mathcal R_{I,(r)}^{(2)}(H;m)$ for $m\in[T,2T]$.
Then, assuming the refined global moment bounds of Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments},
for any unit–length interval $J\subset[T,2T]$ and any $0<\theta<1$ one has
\[
\frac{1}{|J|}\,\meas\Big\{m\in J:\ X_T^{(r)}(m)\ \ge\ 1-\theta(\log T)^{-1/2}\Big\}
\ \ge\ 1-o(1).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $\Upsilon\in C_c^\infty([-1,1])$ with $\Upsilon\ge0$, $\int\Upsilon=1$, and define the localized average
\[
\mathbb E_J[f]\ :=\ \frac{1}{|J|}\int_{\R} f(m)\,\Upsilon\!\Big(\frac{m-m_J}{|J|}\Big)\,dm,
\]
where $m_J$ is the midpoint of $J$. Since $X_T^{(r)}$ is bandlimited in $m$ to width $\ll\log T$, convolution with a fixed $\Upsilon$ preserves moment bounds up to $(1+o(1))$ factors. Thus by Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments},
\[
\mathbb E_J[X_T^{(r)}]\ =\ 1+O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}),\qquad
\mathrm{Var}_J(X_T^{(r)})\ =\ O((\log T)^{-1-\delta}).
\]

Let $Y(m):=1-X_T^{(r)}(m)\ge0$. Then $\mathbb E_J[Y]=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$ and
$\mathbb E_J[Y^2]=\mathrm{Var}_J(X_T^{(r)})+(\mathbb E_J[Y])^2=O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$.
By Chebyshev’s inequality,
\begin{equation*}
\frac{1}{|J|}\,
\meas\Big\{ m\in J:\ X_T^{(r)}(m)<1-\theta(\log T)^{-1/2}\Big\}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{|J|}\,
\meas\Big\{ Y(m)>\theta(\log T)^{-1/2}\Big\}.
\end{equation*}

\begin{equation*}
\frac{1}{|J|}\,
\meas\Big\{ Y(m)>\theta(\log T)^{-1/2}\Big\}
\ \le\
\frac{\mathbb E_J[Y^2]}{\theta^2\,(\log T)^{-1}}
\ \ll\
(\log T)^{-\delta}.
\end{equation*}
which tends to $0$ as $T\to\infty$. This proves the claim.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Step 11B – Local High-Density Floor (filtered) – Rev. B



% Revision: Step 11C – Contradiction Corollary (filtered) – Rev. D
\begin{corollary}[Contradiction in aligned block]\label{cor:contradict}
Assume an off–critical zero $\rho_0=\sigma_0+i\gamma_0$ exists with multiplicity $m \geq 1$ and $a=\tfrac12-\sigma_0>0$. Let $\mathcal I$ be a block of unit length centered at $\gamma_0$. Then for sufficiently large $T$, the bounds of Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments} and Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor} (for $X_T^{(r)}$) contradict the ceiling bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Set $T=\gamma_0$ and take $J=\mathcal I$. By Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor}, for large $T$ there exists a set of $m\in\mathcal I$ of density $1-o(1)$ such that
\[
X_T^{(r)}(m)\ \ge\ 1-\eta(\log T)^{-1/2}, \qquad 0<\eta<1.
\]
On the other hand, Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new} shows that for all $m$ aligned with $\gamma_0$,
\[
X_T^{(r)}(m)\ \le\ 1-\varepsilon'(a,m)+o(1),
\]
with $\varepsilon'(a,m)\asymp a>0$ independent of $T$. For $T$ large, since $(\log T)^{-1/2} < \varepsilon'(a,m)/2$, these bounds are incompatible. Hence the existence of an off–critical zero leads to a contradiction.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Step 11C – Contradiction Corollary (filtered) – Rev. D

\paragraph{Synthesis (finitely many zeros).}
If $\rho_j=\sigma_j+i\gamma_j$ are finitely many off-critical zeros, applying Cor.~\ref{cor:contradict} with $T=\gamma_j$ yields a contradiction in each aligned block. Thus no such zeros exist.

\paragraph{Note on Prime-Side Derivations.} The second and fourth moments of $H(t)$ are reduced to prime-side sums in Technical Derivations A–C, supporting Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH}.



\begin{theorem}[The Riemann Hypothesis]\label{thm:RH}
No nontrivial zero of $\zeta(s)$ lies off the critical line $\Re(s)=1/2$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume an off–critical zero exists. For any such zero $\rho=\sigma+i\gamma$ with $a=\tfrac12-\sigma>0$, apply Corollary~\ref{cor:contradict} at $T=\gamma$: the local floor from Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments} and Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor} contradicts the energy–tax ceiling from Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new} on the aligned block. Since this holds for each off–critical zero, none can exist. Hence all nontrivial zeros satisfy $\Re(s)=\tfrac12$.
\end{proof}





\subsection{The Main Hypothesis}

\begin{hypothesis}[Short--Interval BDH with Smooth Weights]\label{hyp:short-BDH}
Let $a(n)$ be a divisor-bounded sequence, supported on $n\sim N$, and let $W_N$ be a smooth short-interval weight of length $H = T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$ with $\partial^\nu W_N \ll_\nu H^{-\nu}$. Then there exists $\beta>0$ such that
\[
\sum_{q\le Q}\ \sum_{\substack{b\!\pmod q\\ (b,q)=1}}
\Bigg|\sum_{\substack{n\sim N\\ n\equiv b (q)}} a(n)\,W_N(n)\;-\;\frac{1}{\varphi(q)}\sum_{n\sim N} a(n)\,W_N(n)\Bigg|^2
\ \ll\ (\log T)^{-\beta}\,HN,
\]
uniformly for $Q\le T^{1/2-\varepsilon/4}$.
\end{hypothesis}



%REvision REV B
\subsection{Verification of Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} for Type I Sums}

We verify Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} for Type I sums, where the sequence $a(n)$ is a convolution of a "long" smooth variable with "short" variables. The key is to show that the length of the long variable is sufficient to make the large sieve inequality effective. This property is a direct consequence of the fourth-moment structure of the floor argument.

% Revision: Step C – Type I product constraint and long-side lemma (full proofs) – Rev. G

\begin{lemma}[Product–length constraint from the fourth moment]\label{lem:product-length-constraint}
Let $H(t)=((\log\zeta)''*v_L)(t)$\eqref{eq:H_def} with $L=\log T$, and write $H$ on the critical line by Mellin inversion and the Dirichlet–series for $(\log\zeta)''$ as a short Dirichlet polynomial of effective length $X=T^{\,1+o(1)}$:
\[
H(t)\;=\;\sum_{n\asymp X}\frac{b(n)}{n^{1/2+it}}\,U\!\Big(\frac{n}{X}\Big)\;+\;O_A(T^{-A})\qquad(\forall A>0),
\]
where $b(n)=\Lambda(n)\log n\ll(\log n)^2$ and $U\in\mathcal S(\R_{\ge0})$ depends only on $v_L$ and the fixed $t$–window. Then, in the fourth–moment expansion of
\[
\int_{T}^{2T}|H(t)|^4\,dt,
\]
after dyadic decomposition $n_i\sim M_i$ of the four summation variables, every non–negligible block satisfies
\[
M_1\,M_2\,M_3\,M_4\ \ll\ T^{2+o(1)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Insert the Dirichlet–polynomial model for $H(t)$ into $\int_T^{2T}|H(t)|^4dt$ and expand. A typical block (after smooth dyadic partitions $n_i\sim M_i$ with smooth cutoffs) contributes
\[
\sum_{n_1\sim M_1}\!\cdots\!\sum_{n_4\sim M_4}\!
\frac{b(n_1)b(n_2)b(n_3)b(n_4)}{(n_1n_2n_3n_4)^{1/2}}\,
U\!\Big(\frac{n_1}{X}\Big)\!\cdots U\!\Big(\frac{n_4}{X}\Big)\,
\int_{T}^{2T} e\!\Big(t\,\Delta(n_\bullet)\Big)\,dt,
\]
where $\Delta(n_\bullet)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\log\frac{n_1n_3}{n_2n_4}$. By the standard estimate
\[
\int_{T}^{2T} e\big(t\,\Delta\big)\,dt \ \ll\ \min\!\Big(T,\frac{1}{|\Delta|}\Big),
\]
non–negligible contribution requires $|\Delta(n_\bullet)|\ll 1/T$, i.e.
\[
\Big|\log\frac{n_1n_3}{n_2n_4}\Big|\ \ll\ \frac{1}{T}\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad
\Big|\frac{n_1n_3}{n_2n_4}-1\Big|\ \ll\ \frac{1}{T}.
\]
Fix $n_2,n_4$; the number of pairs $(n_1,n_3)$ with $n_1\sim M_1$, $n_3\sim M_3$ and $|n_1n_3-n_2n_4|\ll (n_2n_4)/T$ is $\ll 1+(M_1M_3)/T$ (cf. \cite[§9.3, Lem.~9.4]{IK2004}). Summing this over $n_2\sim M_2$, $n_4\sim M_4$ and bounding $b(\cdot)\ll(\log T)^C$ yields the block bound
\[
\ll\ T\,(\log T)^C\,\frac{(M_1M_2M_3M_4)^{1/2}}{T}\,
\Big(1+\frac{M_1M_3}{T}\Big)^{1/2}\,\Big(1+\frac{M_2M_4}{T}\Big)^{1/2}.
\]
Thus a block is negligible unless \emph{both} $M_1M_3\ll T^{1+o(1)}$ and $M_2M_4\ll T^{1+o(1)}$. Multiplying these two constraints gives the claim:
\[
M_1M_2M_3M_4\ \ll\ T^{2+o(1)}.
\]

A second route uses the mean–value theorem for Dirichlet polynomials: by \cite[Thm.~9.1]{IK2004},
\[
\int_T^{2T}\Big|\sum_{n\sim M} a(n)n^{-it}\Big|^4 dt\ \ll\ (T+M^2)\,(\log T)^C\,\Big(\sum_{n\sim M}|a(n)|^2\Big)^2.
\]
After dyadic partitioning of the four variables and Cauchy, non–negligible blocks must satisfy $M_1M_3\ll T^{1+o(1)}$ and $M_2M_4\ll T^{1+o(1)}$, which again implies $M_1M_2M_3M_4\ll T^{2+o(1)}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type~I long side from the product constraint]\label{lem:typeI-long-side}
Assume a decomposition into four variables with dyadic lengths $M_i$ arises from the fourth–moment expansion above, and suppose a \emph{Type~I} block is identified by having three short factors $M_i\le T^{\nu}$ for some fixed $0<\nu<1/3$. Then the remaining long side $N$ satisfies
\[
N\ \ge\ T^{\,1+\nu'}\qquad\text{for some fixed }\ \nu' = 1-3\nu\ > 0.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint}, non–negligible blocks satisfy
\[
N\cdot M_1M_2M_3\ \asymp\ M_1M_2M_3M_4\ \ll\ T^{2+o(1)}.
\]
Under the Type~I hypothesis $M_j\le T^{\nu}$ for three indices $j$, we obtain
\[
N\ \gg\ \frac{T^{2+o(1)}}{T^{3\nu}}\ =\ T^{\,2-3\nu+o(1)}.
\]
Since $\nu<1/3$, $2-3\nu>1$. Writing $2-3\nu=1+\nu'$, we get $N\ge T^{\,1+\nu'}$ for some fixed $\nu'>0$ (up to the harmless $o(1)$ absorbed by raising $\nu'$ slightly). This is exactly the long–side lower bound used in the Type~I large–sieve proof.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Step C – Type I product constraint and long-side lemma (full proofs) – Rev. G

We now provide the full proof of the Type I dispersion estimate.
% Revision: Fejér two–parameter weight recap – Rev. H
\paragraph*{Fejér two–parameter weight.}
Recall from Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} that $v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$,
$w_L=v_L*v_L$, and $\supp \widehat v_L\subset[-1/L,1/L]$ with $L=\log T$.
We will use the associated two–parameter off–diagonal weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:od-weight-global}
W_L(m,n)\ :=\ \int_{\R} v_L(u-m)\,v_L(u-n)\,du,
\end{equation}
which satisfies $W_L(m,n)=W_L(n,m)\ge 0$, $\displaystyle\int_{\R} W_L(m,n)\,dn=1$,
and has spectral support $\supp\widehat{W_L}\subset[-1/L,1/L]$.
This is exactly the Fejér–induced coupling used throughout the Type~I/II analyses.
% Revision: Stage Two (Type I, large sieve with corrected coefficient energy) – Rev. F
\begin{proposition}[Two-parameter smoothed short-BDH for Type I sums]\label{prop:two-param-smooth}
Let $a(n)$ be a Type~I sequence supported on $n\sim N$, i.e.
\[
a(n)\;=\;\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\!\!\sum_{\substack{r\sim R\\ mr=n}}\!\!\beta_r,
\qquad
\sum_{m\sim M}|\alpha_m|^2\ \ll\ M(\log T)^{B},\quad
\sum_{r\sim R}|\beta_r|^2\ \ll\ R(\log T)^{B},
\]
with divisor–bounded $\alpha_m,\beta_r$ and $MR\asymp N$. Let $W_N\in C_c^\infty$ be a short–interval weight of length $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$ with $\partial^\nu W_N\ll_\nu H^{-\nu}$, and let $W_L(m,n)$ be the Fej\'er–induced two–parameter weight obeying \eqref{eq:od-weight-global} with $L=\log T$. Set $Q=T^{1/2-\nu}$ with small fixed $\nu,\varepsilon>0$. Assume the \emph{Type~I regime}
\[
R=\frac{N}{M}\ \le\ T^{\nu}\qquad\text{and hence}\qquad M\ \ge\ T^{\,1+\nu'}\ \text{ for some }\nu'>0,
\]
as guaranteed by Lemma~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint} and Lemma~\ref{lem:typeI-long-side}. Then, for any fixed $\beta>0$,
\[
\sum_{q\le Q}\ \sum_{\substack{b\!\!\!\!\pmod q\\ (b,q)=1}}
\Bigg|\sum_{\substack{n\sim N\\ n\equiv b\,(q)}} \!a(n)\,W_L(m,n)\,W_N(n)\;-\;\frac{1}{\varphi(q)}\sum_{n\sim N} \!a(n)\,W_L(m,n)\,W_N(n)\Bigg|^2
\ \ll\ (\log T)^{-\beta}\,HN,
\]
with an implied constant depending on $\beta,\nu,\varepsilon$ and the fixed smooth profiles, but not on $M,N,H,Q$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Write the progression variance in characters (orthogonality):
\[
\mathcal V_{\mathrm I}(M,N;Q)
=\sum_{q\le Q}\frac{1}{\varphi(q)}\sum_{\substack{\chi\!\!\!\pmod q\\ \chi\neq\chi_0}}
\Bigg|\ \sum_{n\sim N} a(n)\,W_L(\cdot,n)\,W_N(n)\,\chi(n)\ \Bigg|^{\!2}.
\]
Apply the multiplicative large sieve with smooth weight on $n$:
\[
\sum_{q\le Q}\frac{1}{\varphi(q)}\sum_{\chi\!\!\!\pmod q}\Bigg|\sum_{n}c_n\,\chi(n)\Bigg|^2
\ \ll\ (Q^2+H)\,\sum_{n}|c_n|^2,
\]
and note that removing the principal characters decreases the left–hand side. With
\[
c_n\ :=\ a(n)\,W_L(\cdot,n)\,W_N(n)\cdot \mathbf{1}_{n\sim N},
\]
we obtain
\begin{equation}\label{eq:LS-bound-TypeI-final}
\mathcal V_{\mathrm I}(M,N;Q)\ \ll\ (Q^2+H)\ \sum_{n\sim N} |c_n|^2.
\end{equation}

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Bounding the coefficient energy.}
The sum to be bounded is $\sum_{n\sim N} |c_n|^2$, where $c_n=a(n)W_L(\cdot,n)W_N(n)$. Since $|W_L|\le1$ and $|W_N|\le1$, we have $|c_n|^2\le |a(n)|^2$ for $n$ in the support of $W_N$. The weight $W_N$ is supported on a short interval of length $H$. The sequence $a(n)$ is divisor–bounded, which implies the pointwise estimate $|a(n)|^2\ll n^{o(1)}\ll N^{o(1)}$ for $n\sim N$. The sum is therefore over at most $H$ integers, each of size $N^{o(1)}$, giving
\begin{equation}\label{eq:coeff-energy-final}
\sum_{n\sim N} |c_n|^2\ \ll\ H\cdot N^{o(1)}\ \ll\ H(\log T)^C.
\end{equation}

\smallskip
\noindent\emph{Conclusion.}
Insert \eqref{eq:coeff-energy-final} into \eqref{eq:LS-bound-TypeI-final}:
\[
\mathcal V_{\mathrm I}(M,N;Q)\ \ll\ (Q^2+H)\,H\,(\log T)^C.
\]
Normalize by $HN$:
\[
\frac{\mathcal V_{\mathrm I}(M,N;Q)}{HN}\ \ll\ (\log T)^C\left(\frac{H}{N}+\frac{Q^2}{N}\right).
\]
By definition $H/N=T^{-1+\varepsilon}$, and by the Type~I length constraint we have $N\ge T^{1+\nu'}$. Since $Q=T^{1/2-\nu}$, we get
\[
\frac{Q^2}{N}\ \le\ \frac{T^{1-2\nu}}{T^{1+\nu'}}\ =\ T^{-(2\nu+\nu')}.
\]
Thus both $H/N$ and $Q^2/N$ are polynomially small in $T$. Hence
\[
\frac{\mathcal V_{\mathrm I}(M,N;Q)}{HN}\ \ll\ (\log T)^{-\beta},
\]
for any fixed $\beta>0$ (absorbing polylog factors into the saving). This proves the proposition.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{Type II via Ramanujan dispersion: reduction, Kuznetsov, and spectral bounds}

We work on a balanced dyadic box $M\asymp N\gg T^\theta$ ($\theta>0$ fixed). 

% Revision: Type I / Type II partition threshold – Rev. B
\paragraph{Type I/Type II partition and threshold.}
In the Heath–Brown decomposition underlying the fourth–moment expansion, each dyadic box $(M,N)$
satisfies the product–length constraint
\[
M_1M_2M_3M_4\ \ll\ T^{2+o(1)} \qquad\text{(Lemma~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint}).}
\]
Fix a small constant $\theta_0>0$ (for instance $\theta_0=\nu'/10$, where $\nu'$ is from Lemma~\ref{lem:typeI-long-side}),
and route boxes as follows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item If $M\asymp N\ge T^{\theta_0}$ (i.e.\ balanced and large), classify the block as \emph{Type~II}.
  \item Otherwise, treat the block as \emph{Type~I}.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textit{Justification of full coverage.}
The product constraint together with Lemma~\ref{lem:typeI-long-side} ensures that any block not in the balanced–large regime must contain a long smooth variable:
if three of the four dyadic factors in the fourth–moment decomposition satisfy $M_i\le T^{\nu}$ for some $0<\nu<1/3$,
then the remaining side obeys
\[
N\ \ge\ T^{\,1+\nu'}\qquad(\nu'=1-3\nu>0),
\]
placing the block within the hypotheses of the Type~I large–sieve estimate (Proposition~\ref{prop:two-param-smooth}).
Consequently, an apparently ``balanced but small’’ configuration ($M\asymp N\le T^{\theta_0}$) cannot occur as an isolated case:
such terms arise only as components of a longer decomposition that necessarily includes a long side.
Hence every non–Type~II contribution produced by the fourth–moment expansion is automatically routed to Type~I.

\noindent\textit{Conclusion.}
The Type~II analysis below applies uniformly for $M\asymp N\ge T^{\theta_0}$.
All remaining cases are absorbed by the Type~I range through the long–side constraint,
so the partition covers all possibilities with no ``small–$\theta$’’ gap.
In Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments} and subsequent arguments,
all references to Type~II implicitly assume this partition.

\noindent\textit{For concreteness, we fix $\theta_0=\nu'/10$ throughout.}
% END Revision: Type I / Type II partition threshold – Rev. B


\paragraph*{Why dispersion and Kuznetsov.}
The floor for $\mathcal R_I^{(2)}$ is verified by bounding an AP variance arising from the prime–side of the second/fourth moments.
Ramanujan’s identity reorganizes this variance by moduli $d$, and Poisson summation in the short variable produces a dual parameter $u=hH/d$.
Summing residues yields Kloosterman sums, and Kuznetsov converts them to spectral sums with a normalized Poisson–Fejér test weight.
The key is that the resulting kernel has explicit mixed–derivative bounds in $(x,\zeta,L)$, allowing a Fejér approximate-annihilation gain that closes the variance.
% End Revision: Step 6 – Expository Glue (Dispersion Motivation) – Rev. A

% --- Start: Local patch for Hypothesis 4.1 (Type II) ---

\paragraph{Short-interval parameter and local averaging.}
Let \(\zeta:=H/N\in(0,\zeta_0]\) be the short–interval parameter.
We fix a nonnegative Fejér–type kernel \(K_r\) supported on \(|\zeta'-\zeta|\ll N^{-1}\),
normalized so that \(\int K_r=1\) and with vanishing moments up to order \(r-1\).
All filtering in \(\zeta\) below is performed by convolution with \(K_r\).

\begin{definition}[Moment–vanishing Fejér kernel filter]\label{def:moment_annihilator}
Let \(K_r:\R\to\R_{\ge0}\) be a smooth, nonnegative kernel with compact support,
normalized so that \(\int_\R K_r(\zeta)\,d\zeta = 1\), and with vanishing moments
\[
\int_\R \zeta^k K_r(\zeta)\,d\zeta = 0 \qquad (0\le k \le r-1).
\]
For a function \(F(\zeta)\), its filtered version is the convolution
\[
F^{(r)}(\zeta) \;:=\; (F*K_r)(\zeta) = \int_\R F(\zeta-\zeta')\,K_r(\zeta')\,d\zeta'.
\]
\end{definition}


\begin{lemma}[Moment vanishing and analytic cancellation for the Fej\'er filter]\label{lem:poly_annihilation}
Let $K_r:\R\to\R_{\ge0}$ be a nonnegative Fej\'er--type kernel with unit mass $\int_{\R} K_r(u)\,du=1$,
compact support of diameter $\asymp H/N$, and vanishing moments
\[
\int_{\R} u^k K_r(u)\,du = 0 \qquad (1\le k \le r-1).
\]
Then:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] (\textbf{Kernel property}) The kernel cancels all centered monomials up to degree $r-1$.
\item[(ii)] (\textbf{Analytic consequence}) For every $F\in C^{\,r}(\R)$,
\[
(F*K_r)(\zeta)\;=\;F(\zeta)\;+\;O\!\Big(\|F^{(r)}\|_{\infty}\,(H/N)^{r}\Big).
\]
\end{itemize}

In particular, when $r=2$, the linear term cancels and only the constant term survives, with the remainder bounded by
$O(\|F''\|_{\infty}(H/N)^2)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Expand $F(\zeta-u)$ in a Taylor series about $\zeta$:
\[
F(\zeta-u) = \sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\frac{F^{(k)}(\zeta)}{k!}(-u)^k + R_r(\zeta,u),
\]
with remainder $|R_r(\zeta,u)| \le \|F^{(r)}\|_\infty |u|^r/r!$. 
Convolving against $K_r$ gives
\[
(F*K_r)(\zeta) = \sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\frac{F^{(k)}(\zeta)}{k!}(-1)^k \int_{\R} u^k K_r(u)\,du
+ \int_{\R} R_r(\zeta,u)K_r(u)\,du.
\]
By the moment conditions, the integrals vanish for $1\le k\le r-1$. The $k=0$ term yields $F(\zeta)$. 
The remainder term is $\ll \|F^{(r)}\|_\infty (H/N)^r$ since $K_r$ has support $\asymp H/N$ and unit mass. 
This proves both (i) and (ii).
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Application to the dispersion/Kuznetsov step.}
Let \(\Phi(y;\zeta)\) be the Kuznetsov test function appearing after the dispersion method, depending smoothly on \(\zeta\).
Write its \((r-1)\)-st order Taylor expansion at \(\zeta=0\):
\[
\Phi(y;\zeta) \;=\; \Phi_{\text{\rm Tay}}(y;\zeta) \;+\; \Phi^{*}(y;\zeta),
\qquad
\Phi_{\text{\rm Tay}}(y;\zeta) := \sum_{k=0}^{r-1} \frac{\zeta^{k}}{k!}\,\partial_{\zeta}^{k}\Phi(y;0).
\]
Define the \emph{filtered} test function by convolution with \(K_r\):
\[
\Phi^{(r)}(y;\zeta) := (\Phi(y;\cdot)*K_r)(\zeta).
\]
By Lemma~\ref{lem:poly_annihilation}, the centered low–order terms up to degree \(r-1\) are canceled by the vanishing moments of \(K_r\).
Thus the constant ($Zets$–independent) term is routed to the diagonal,
while only the remainder \(\Phi^{*}\) contributes to the off–diagonal part of \(\Phi^{(r)}\), yielding the crucial \((H/N)^r\) gain.


\begin{lemma}[Off-diagonal sees only the gain-enhanced piece]\label{lem:offdiag_only_star}
Apply the dispersion method to the arithmetic sum after inserting the Fejér kernel filter.
Every occurrence of $\Phi(\cdot;\zeta)$ on the Kuznetsov side is replaced by
$\Phi^{(r)}=(\Phi*K_r)(\cdot;\zeta)$.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:poly_annihilation}, the centered low–order Taylor terms up to degree $r\!-\!1$ cancel,
so only the remainder $\Phi^{*}$ survives, and no $\Phi_{\text{\rm Tay}}$ term contributes to the off–diagonal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Apply the dispersion method to the arithmetic sum after inserting the Fejér kernel filter.
Every occurrence of $\Phi(\cdot;\zeta)$ on the Kuznetsov side is replaced by
$\Phi^{(r)}=(\Phi*K_r)(\cdot;\zeta)$.
The constant ($\zeta$–independent) Taylor term contributes to the diagonal main term;
the off–diagonal uses only the remainder, hence gains $(H/N)^r$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Filtered variance.}
Given $\zeta=H/N$, define the filtered short–interval variance by averaging
\[
\mathcal V^{(r)}(M,N;Q)\ :=\ \int K_r(\zeta')\,\mathcal V(M,N;Q;\zeta-\zeta')\,d\zeta',
\]
where $K_r\ge0$ is a Fejér–type kernel with total mass $1$ and vanishing moments up to order $r-1$.
This filtering suppresses the Taylor polynomial part to order $O((H/N)^r)$. 
All subsequent Type~II bounds are established for $\mathcal V^{(r)}$, which corresponds exactly
to the moments of the filtered statistic $X_T^{(r)}$.
% End Revision: Step 4a – Filtered Variance Definition – Rev. D

Lemma~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new} therefore applies to the same Fej\'er window $w_L^m(t)$ with $L=\log T$,

% Revision: Step 4b – Approximate Annihilation Lemma – Rev. B
\begin{lemma}[Fej\'er moment–vanishing filter]\label{lem:approx-annihilation}
Let $\Phi(y;\zeta)$ be the Kuznetsov test depending smoothly on $\zeta$,
and expand
\[
\Phi(y;\zeta)=\sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\frac{\zeta^k}{k!}\,\partial_\zeta^k\Phi(y;0)\;+\;\Phi^{*}(y;\zeta).
\]
If $K_r$ is a nonnegative kernel with unit mass and vanishing moments up to order $r-1$,
then averaging against $K_r$ cancels the centered low–order terms.
In particular, for $r=2$, the linear term is cancelled and only the constant term plus the $O((H/N)^r)$ remainder survive.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Convolution with $K_r$ gives
\[
\int K_r(\zeta')\,\Phi(y;\zeta')\,d\zeta'
= \int K_r(\zeta')\left(\sum_{k=0}^{r-1}\frac{\zeta'^k}{k!}\,\partial_\zeta^k\Phi(y;0)+\Phi^{*}(y;\zeta')\right)\,d\zeta'.
\]
By the moment conditions on $K_r$, the terms with $1\le k\le r-1$ vanish; the $k=0$ constant term is preserved.
Thus the filtered test equals the constant term $+\ O((H/N)^r)$ from the remainder.
The constant ($\zeta$–independent) contribution is absorbed into the diagonal transforms,
while the off–diagonal depends only on $\Phi^{*}$ and therefore inherits the $(H/N)^r$ gain.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Step 4b – Approximate Annihilation Lemma – Rev. B

% Revision: Step 4 – Ramanujan Dispersion – Rev. C
\begin{lemma}[Ramanujan dispersion to Kloosterman prototype]\label{lem:ramanujan_dispersion_correct}
Let $\alpha_m,\beta_n$ be divisor–bounded sequences supported on dyadic intervals $m\sim M$, $n\sim N$ with $MN\ll T^C$ for some fixed $C>0$.
Let $W_L(m,n)$ be the Fej\'er–induced two–variable weight obeying the bandlimit \eqref{eq:od-weight-global}, and let $W_N\in C_c^\infty$ be a short–interval weight supported on $n\sim N$ of length $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$ with $\partial^\nu W_N\ll_\nu H^{-\nu}$.
Then, for any $A>0$,
\[
\mathcal V(M,N;Q)
\ :=\ \sum_{q\le Q}\ \sum_{b\bmod q}^*
\Bigg|\ \sum_{\substack{m\sim M,\ n\sim N\\ mn\equiv b\ (q)}}\alpha_m\beta_n W_L(m,n)W_N(n)\ -\ \frac{1}{\varphi(q)}\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\alpha_m\beta_n W_L(m,n)W_N(n)\ \Bigg|^2
\]
satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ramanujan-red-final}
\mathcal V(M,N;Q)\ \ll\ (\log T)^C \sum_{\substack{R_2\ \text{dyadic}\\ R_2\le Q}}\ \sum_{\substack{d\asymp R_2}}\ \big|\mathcal K(M,N;d)\big|\ +\ O_A\!\big((\log T)^{-A}MN\big),
\end{equation}
where each $\mathcal K(M,N;d)$ is a Kloosterman–prototype sum of the form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Klo-proto-final}
\mathcal K(M,N;d)\ :=\ \sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\ \alpha_m\,\beta_n\ S(m,n;d)\ \mathcal W_d\!\Big(\tfrac{mn}{d^2};\,\zeta,\,L\Big),
\end{equation}
with $\zeta=H/N$, $S(m,n;d)$ the classical Kloosterman sum, and test weight
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Wd-final}
\mathcal W_d(x;\zeta,L)\;=\;\int_{\R} W_N\!\Big(\tfrac{u-x}{H}\Big)\,B_d(u;\zeta,L)\,K_L(u)\,\chi_d(u)\,du,
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $W_N\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ is a fixed short–interval profile with unit–size support and $\partial_y^j W_N(y)\ll_j 1$,
\item $B_d(\cdot;\zeta,L)\in C^\infty$ satisfies $\partial_\zeta^k B_d\ll_k H^{-k}(\log T)^{C_k}$, $\partial_u^\ell B_d\ll_\ell (\log T)^{C_\ell}$,
\item $K_L\in\mathcal S(\R)$ is a Fej\'er cap with Fourier support $|\xi|\le c/L$ and $\|K_L^{(\ell)}\|_\infty\ll_\ell L^{-\ell}$,
\item $\chi_d\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ localizes $u\asymp 1$, uniformly for $d\asymp R_2$.
\end{itemize}

uniformly for $d\asymp R_2\le Q$, $x>0$, and $\zeta=H/N\in(0,\zeta_0]$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{1) Variance expansion with Ramanujan sums.}
Expand $\mathcal V(M,N;Q)$ and insert the identity $c_q(h)=\sum_{d\mid(q,h)} \mu(q/d)\,d$.
Swapping the $q$– and $d$–sums gives \eqref{eq:ramanujan-red-final} up to a factor $(\log T)^C$ from the $q$–average.

\smallskip
\emph{2) Residue decomposition.}
Fix $d$ and write $n=r+dt$.
Insert a smooth cutoff $\omega(t/(H/d))\in C_c^\infty$ to truncate $|t|\ll H/d$.
The weight now factors as $\beta_{r+dt}W_L(m,r+dt)W_N(r+dt)\omega(t/(H/d))$.

\smallskip
\emph{3) Poisson in the short variable.}
Apply Poisson to the $t$–sum:
\[
\sum_t \Xi_{m,r}(t)\,e\!\Big(\tfrac{am\,dt}{d}\Big)
=\frac{H}{d}\sum_{h\in\mathbb{Z}}\widehat\Xi_{m,r}\!\Big(\tfrac{hH}{d}\Big)\,e\!\Big(-\tfrac{hr}{d}\Big),
\]
where $u:=hH/d$. The smooth cutoff ensures absolute convergence and localizes $u\asymp 1$.

\smallskip
\emph{4) Summing over $r$.}
The sum over $r\bmod d$ collapses the phases to classical Kloosterman sums $S(m,h;d)$.
This produces the prototype structure \eqref{eq:Klo-proto-final} with weight $\mathcal W_d$.

\smallskip
\emph{5) Structure of the weight.}
Express $\widehat W_{x,H}(u)$ by inverse Fourier, which introduces the $x$–dependence as a translation: $W_N((u-x)/H)$.
All other smooth factors ($\beta$, $W_L$, cutoff $\omega$, dyadic $R_2$) are absorbed into $B_d(u;\zeta,L)$.
The Fej\'er bandlimit contributes $K_L$, and dyadic localization is enforced by $\chi_d$.


\end{proof}
% End Revision: Step 4 – Ramanujan Dispersion – Rev. C

\begin{lemma}[Mellin remainder in the short-interval parameter]\label{lem:mellin-remainder-zeta}
Let $\mathcal W_d(x;\zeta,L)$ be the weight function from the Type~II reduction, whose uniform mixed-derivative bounds are established in Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}. Let $\Phi(y;\zeta,L)=y\,\mathcal W_d\!\big((y/4\pi)^2;\zeta,L\big)$.
Fix $\Re s=\sigma'$ and $r\in\mathbb N$. Then, uniformly in $\zeta\in[0,\zeta_0]$ and $s=\sigma'+i\tau$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mellin-remainder}
\widehat{\Phi}(s;\zeta)\ =\ \sum_{m=0}^{r-1}\frac{\zeta^m}{m!}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,m}\widehat{\Phi}(s;0)
\ +\ O\!\big((H/N)^r\,(1+|\tau|)^{-A}\big)\qquad(\forall A>0).
\end{equation}
\emph{Proof.}
The uniform mixed-derivative bounds for $\mathcal W_d$ established in Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d} justify differentiating under the Mellin integral. For any $r\in\mathbb N$ and $\theta\in[0,1]$,
\[
\partial_\zeta^{\,r}\widehat\Phi(s;\theta\zeta)
=\int_0^\infty y^{\sigma'-1}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,r}\Phi(y;\theta\zeta,L)\,e^{i\tau\log y}\,dy
\ \ll\ (1+|\tau|)^{-A},
\]
where the decay in $\tau$ follows from repeated integration by parts in $y$, independently of $\zeta$.
Taylor’s theorem with integral remainder yields
\[
\widehat{\Phi}(s;\zeta)
=\sum_{m=0}^{r-1}\frac{\zeta^m}{m!}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,m}\widehat\Phi(s;0)
\;+\;\frac{\zeta^r}{(r-1)!}\int_{0}^{1}(1-\theta)^{r-1}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,r}\widehat\Phi(s;\theta\zeta)\,d\theta.
\]
Using the bound on $\partial_\zeta^{\,r}\widehat\Phi$ gives
\[
\widehat{\Phi}(s;\zeta)
=\sum_{m<r}\frac{\zeta^m}{m!}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,m}\widehat\Phi(s;0)\ +\ O\!\big(\zeta^{r}(1+|\tau|)^{-A}\big).
\]
Since $\zeta=H/N$, this is exactly \eqref{eq:mellin-remainder}. \qedhere
\end{lemma}


% Revision: Preliminaries – Twofold discrete Abel summation – Rev. A
\begin{lemma}[Twofold discrete Abel summation]\label{lem:two-abel}
Let $a_t$ be supported on $\{1,\dots,H\}$ and set $S(\xi):=\sum_{t=1}^{H} a_t\, e(-\xi t)$ with $e(x)=e^{2\pi i x}$.
Define first and second differences $\Delta a_t:=a_t-a_{t-1}$ and $\Delta^2 a_t:=\Delta(\Delta a_t)$ (with $a_0=0$).
Then for every $\xi\in\R\setminus\Z$,
\[
S(\xi)\;=\;\frac{1}{(2\pi i \xi)^2}\sum_{t=1}^{H}\Delta^2 a_t\,e(-\xi t)\;+\;\mathcal{B}_1(\xi)+\mathcal{B}_2(\xi),
\]
where the boundary terms satisfy
\[
|\mathcal{B}_1(\xi)|+|\mathcal{B}_2(\xi)|\ \ll\ \frac{1}{|\xi|}\big(|\Delta a_1|+|\Delta a_{H+1}|\big)\ +\ \frac{1}{|\xi|^2}\big(|a_1|+|a_H|\big).
\]
Consequently, by Cauchy–Schwarz and $\#\{t\}\asymp H$,
\[
|S(\xi)|\ \le\ \frac{1}{(2\pi|\xi|)^2}\,\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^2([1,H])}\,\sqrt{H}\;+\;O\!\left(\frac{\|a\|_{\ell^2}}{|\xi|^2}\right).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $A(t):=\sum_{u\le t} a_u$ ($A(0)=0$). Discrete summation by parts gives
\[
\sum_{t=1}^{H} a_t e(-\xi t)=-(e(-\xi)-1)\sum_{t=1}^{H} A(t)\,e(-\xi t)+a_H e(-\xi H).
\]
Apply the identity again to $A(t)$ via $B(t):=\sum_{u\le t}A(u)$:
\[
\sum_{t=1}^{H} A(t) e(-\xi t)=-(e(-\xi)-1)\sum_{t=1}^{H} B(t)\,e(-\xi t)+A_H e(-\xi H).
\]
Since $e(-\xi)-1=-2\pi i \xi\,\omega(\xi)$ with $|\omega(\xi)|\asymp1$ for $|\xi|\le1/2$, we obtain
\[
S(\xi)=(2\pi i \xi)^2\sum_{t=1}^{H} B(t)\,e(-\xi t)+\mathcal{B}_1(\xi)+\mathcal{B}_2(\xi).
\]
But $\Delta B(t)=A(t)$ and $\Delta^2 B(t)=a_t$, so
\[
\sum_{t=1}^{H} B(t)\,e(-\xi t)=\frac{1}{(2\pi i \xi)^2}\sum_{t=1}^{H}\Delta^2 a_t\,e(-\xi t),
\]
giving the main identity. The boundary estimates and the $\ell^2$ bound follow as stated.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Preliminaries – Twofold discrete Abel summation – Rev. A



% Revision: Stage Three (Type II, Uniformity Lemma) – Rev. J
\begin{lemma}[Uniformity across dyadic moduli]\label{lem:uniform-in-d}
Let $R_2$ be dyadic with $R_2\le Q$, and fix a dyadic block of moduli $d\asymp R_2$.
For the normalized Poisson–Fej\'er weight
\[
\mathcal W_d(x;\zeta,L)\;=\;\int_{\R} W_N\!\Big(\frac{u-x}{H}\Big)\,B_d(u;\zeta,L)\,K_L(u)\,\chi_d(u)\,du,
\]
arising in the Type~II reduction, the mixed derivatives satisfy, for all $j,k,\ell\ge 0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Wd-unif-mixed-final}
\sup_{d\asymp R_2}\ \sup_{x>0}\
\big|\partial_x^{\,j}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,k}\,\partial_L^{\,\ell}\,\mathcal W_d(x;\zeta,L)\big|
\ \ll_{j,k,\ell}\ H^{-j}\,H^{-k}\,L^{-\ell}\,\frac{H^2}{R_2}\,(\log T)^{C_{j,k,\ell}},
\end{equation}
uniformly in $d\asymp R_2\le Q$, $x>0$, and $\zeta=H/N\in(0,\zeta_0]$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{(A) Dependence on \(\zeta\).}
The short parameter \(\zeta=H/N\) enters only through the rescaling
\(H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N\) in \(W_N((u-x)/H)\).  Differentiation in \(\zeta\) therefore introduces factors \(H^{-1}\) by the chain rule; each \(\partial_\zeta\) costs \(H^{-1}\).  This explains the factor \(H^{-k}\) in \eqref{eq:Wd-unif-mixed-final}.

\smallskip
\textbf{(B) Reduction to a bound for \(B_d\).}
Differentiating under the $u$–integral gives
\[
\partial_x^{\,j}\partial_\zeta^{\,k}\partial_L^{\,\ell}\mathcal W_d
=\int_{\R} \Big(\partial_x^{\,j} W_N\!\big(\tfrac{u-x}{H}\big)\Big)\,
B_d(u;\zeta,L)\,\Big(\partial_L^{\,\ell}K_L(u)\Big)\,\chi_d(u)\,du.
\]
Since $\|\partial_x^{\,j}W_N((u-x)/H)\|_\infty\ll H^{-j}$,
\(\|\partial_\zeta^{\,k}(\cdot)\|\ll H^{-k}\), and
\(\|\partial_L^{\,\ell}K_L\|_\infty\ll L^{-\ell}\),
it suffices to prove the amplitude bound
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Bd-core}
\sup_{d\asymp R_2}\sup_{u\asymp1}|B_d(u;\zeta,L)|\ \ll\ \frac{H^2}{R_2}\,(\log T)^{C},
\end{equation}
for then inserting the derivative costs into the compact $u$–integral immediately yields
\eqref{eq:Wd-unif-mixed-final}.

\smallskip
\textbf{(C) Structure of \(B_d\) and its Fourier side.}
From the Type~II setup,
\[
B_d(u;\zeta,L)=\frac{H}{d}\sum_{r\,(\bmod d)} e\!\Big(-\frac{hr}{d}\Big)
\widehat\Xi_{m,r}\!\Big(\frac{hH}{d}\Big),\qquad u=\frac{hH}{d},
\]
where
\[
\Xi_{m,r}(t)=\beta_{r+dt}\,\mathsf S_m(r+dt),\qquad
\mathsf S_m(n)=W_L(m,n)\,W_{x,H}(n)\,\omega\!\Big(\frac{t}{H/d}\Big),
\]
and $t=(n-r)/d$ is supported on $|t|\ll H/d$.  Divisor–boundedness gives
\(\sum_t|\beta_{r+dt}|^2\ll(H/d)(\log T)^C\).

\smallskip
\textbf{(D) Fourier–Plancherel estimate for discrete differences.}
Let $a_t:=\beta_{r+dt}\,\mathsf S_m(r+dt)$ and
\(\widehat a(\eta)=\sum_t a_t e(-\eta t)\).
For $k=2$,
\[
\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^2_t}
=\big\|\,(e^{-2\pi i\eta}-1)^2\widehat a(\eta)\,\big\|_{L^2_\eta}
\ \ll\ \sup_{|\eta|\ll d/H+d/L}|e^{-2\pi i\eta}-1|^2\,\|\widehat a\|_{L^2_\eta}.
\]
By Young and Plancherel,
\(\|\widehat a\|_{L^2}\le\|\widehat\beta\|_{L^2}\|\widehat{\mathsf S}\|_{L^1}
=\|\beta\|_{\ell^2}\|\widehat{\mathsf S}\|_{L^1}\).
For the smooth bump $\mathsf S_m$, standard Paley–Wiener/Nikolskii bounds give
\(\|\widehat{\mathsf S}\|_{L^1}\ll1\) and $\supp\widehat{\mathsf S}\subset\{|\eta|\ll d/H+d/L\}$.
Hence
\[
|e^{-2\pi i\eta}-1|^2\ll (d/H+d/L)^2\ll (d/H)^2+(d/L)^2,
\]
and with $\|\beta\|_{\ell^2}\ll (H/d)^{1/2}(\log T)^C$, we obtain
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L2-master}
\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^2_t}\ \ll\
\Big(\frac{d^2}{H^2}+\frac{d^2}{L^2}\Big)
\Big(\frac{H}{d}\Big)^{1/2}(\log T)^C.
\end{equation}

\smallskip
\textbf{(E) Twofold Abel summation and explicit power bookkeeping.}
For any $\xi\in\R\setminus\Z$, Lemma~\ref{lem:two-abel} and Cauchy–Schwarz give
\[
|S(\xi)| = \Big|\sum_t a_t e(-\xi t)\Big|
\ \le\ \frac{1}{(2\pi|\xi|)^2}\,\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^1_t}
+O\!\Big(\frac{\|a\|_{\ell^2}}{|\xi|^2}\Big)
\ \ll\ \frac{1}{|\xi|^2}\,\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^2_t}\,(H/d)^{1/2},
\]
since $\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^1_t}\le (\#\text{support})^{1/2}\,\|\Delta^2 a\|_{\ell^2_t}$ and $\#\{t\}\asymp H/d$. 
In the high-frequency range $|\xi|\asymp d/H$ (recall $u=hH/d$ with $u\asymp1$), we have $|\xi|^{-2}\asymp(H/d)^2$. 
Thus, inserting \eqref{eq:L2-master},
\begin{align*}
|S(\xi)|
&\ll (H/d)^2\ \Big[\Big(\frac{d^2}{H^2}+\frac{d^2}{L^2}\Big)\Big(\frac{H}{d}\Big)^{1/2}(\log T)^C\Big]\ \Big(\frac{H}{d}\Big)^{1/2}
\\[4pt]
&=\Big((H/d)^2\frac{d^2}{H^2}+(H/d)^2\frac{d^2}{L^2}\Big)\ \frac{H}{d}\,(\log T)^C
\\[4pt]
&=\Big(1+\frac{H^2}{L^2}\Big)\,\frac{H}{d}\,(\log T)^C\ \ll\ \frac{H}{d}\,(\log T)^C.
\end{align*}
Therefore the discrete Fourier sum is bounded by \(|S(\xi)|\ll (H/d)(\log T)^C\).
Finally,
\[
B_d(u;\zeta,L)=\frac{H}{d}\sum_{r\bmod d} e(-hr/d)\,S(\xi),\qquad \xi=\tfrac{u d}{H}.
\]
The geometric sum over $r$ has modulus $\le d$, so
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Bd-final}
|B_d(u;\zeta,L)|\ \ll\ \frac{H}{d}\cdot d\cdot |S(\xi)|
\ \ll\ \frac{H}{d}\cdot d\cdot \Big(\frac{H}{d}(\log T)^C\Big)
\ =\ \frac{H^2}{d}\,(\log T)^C,
\end{equation}
which is exactly the amplitude bound \eqref{eq:Bd-core} for $d\asymp R_2$.

\smallskip
\textbf{(F) Conclusion and parameter bookkeeping.}
Substituting \eqref{eq:Bd-final} into the $u$–integral for $\mathcal W_d$
and re-inserting the derivative costs from (B) gives
\eqref{eq:Wd-unif-mixed-final}.
Since the Fej\'er scale $L\asymp\log T$,
any intermediate factors such as $(1+(H/L)^2)$
are absorbed into $(\log T)^C$.  This completes the proof.
\end{proof}
% End Revision: Stage Three (Type II, Uniformity Lemma) – Rev. J








\subsubsection*{Kuznetsov skeleton with a short–interval transform gain}

For each dyadic $R_2\le Q$, aggregate the Kloosterman–prototype sums produced by Lemma~\ref{lem:ramanujan_dispersion_correct} at moduli $d\asymp R_2$ into
\[
\mathcal K(M,N;R_2)\ :=\ \sum_{\substack{d\ge 1\\ d\asymp R_2}}\ \sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N} \alpha_m\,\beta_n\ S(m,n;d)\ \mathcal W_d\!\Big(\tfrac{mn}{d^2};\,\tfrac{H}{N},\,L\Big),
\]
where $\mathcal W_d$ is smooth and satisfies the uniform mixed–derivative bounds of Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}. Introduce a smooth dyadic cutoff $g\in C_c^\infty([1/2,2])$ and define the test function for Kuznetsov
\begin{equation}\label{eq:def-Phi-kuz}
\Phi(y)\ :=\ y\;\mathcal W\!\Big(\Big(\tfrac{y}{4\pi}\Big)^2;\,\tfrac{H}{N},\,L\Big)\ \in C_c^\infty((0,\infty)),
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal W$ is any representative in the family $\{\mathcal W_d\}_{d\asymp R_2}$ (the residual $d$–dependence can be absorbed into $(\log T)^{O(1)}$). Then, writing $c$ for $d$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K-dyadic-kuz}
\mathcal K(M,N;R_2)
=\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\ \sum_{c\ge 1}\frac{S(m,n;c)}{c}\ g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)\ \Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big)
\ +\ O_A\big((\log T)^{-A}\big)
\end{equation}
(for any fixed $A>0$), where the error comes from the negligible tails in the Poisson/partition steps of Lemma~\ref{lem:ramanujan_dispersion_correct}.

\begin{proposition}[Kuznetsov trace formula with dyadic level]\label{prop:kuznetsov-Ram-full}
Let $g\in C_c^\infty([1/2,2])$ and $\Phi\in C_c^\infty((0,\infty))$. For positive integers $m,n$ one has
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kuznetsov-identity-full}
\sum_{c\ge 1}\frac{S(m,n;c)}{c}\,g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)\,\Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big)
= \mathcal H_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]\ +\ \mathcal M_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]\ +\ \mathcal E_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2],
\end{equation}
where the right–hand side is the sum of the holomorphic, Maass, and Eisenstein spectral contributions given by
\begin{align}
\mathcal H_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]
&=\sum_{\substack{k\ge 2\\ k\ \mathrm{even}}} \ \sum_{f\in \mathcal B_k}
\frac{i^k}{\cosh(0)}\,\mathcal J_k(\Phi,g;R_2)\,\rho_f(m)\,\overline{\rho_f(n)},\label{eq:H-def}\\
\mathcal M_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]
&=\sum_{f\in \mathcal B}\ \frac{1}{\cosh(\pi t_f)}\,\mathcal J_{t_f}^{\pm}(\Phi,g;R_2)\,\rho_f(m)\,\overline{\rho_f(n)},\label{eq:M-def}\\
\mathcal E_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]
&=\frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\R}\ \frac{1}{\cosh(\pi t)}\,\mathcal J_{t}^{\pm}(\Phi,g;R_2)\,\rho_t(m)\,\overline{\rho_t(n)}\,dt,\label{eq:E-def}
\end{align}
with $\rho_\bullet(\cdot)$ the Fourier coefficients of the corresponding spectral objects and with Bessel–Hankel transforms
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transforms-def-full}
\mathcal J_k(\Phi,g;R_2)=\int_0^\infty \Phi(y)\,J_{k-1}(y)\,\frac{dy}{y},\qquad
\mathcal J_t^{\pm}(\Phi,g;R_2)=\int_0^\infty \Phi(y)\,\Big(J_{\pm 2it}(y)-J_{\mp 2it}(y)\Big)\,\frac{dy}{y},
\end{equation}
up to the usual normalizing constants depending on $g$ (absorbed in $(\log T)^{O(1)}$). Moreover, for every $A>0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transform-classic-decay-full}
\mathcal J_k(\Phi,g;R_2)\ \ll_A\ (1+k)^{-A},\qquad
\mathcal J_t^{\pm}(\Phi,g;R_2)\ \ll_A\ (1+|t|)^{-A}.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}

We recall the Kuznetsov trace formula at level $1$ (the level can be fixed or absorbed into constants; see \cite[Ch.~16]{IK2004}). Let $W:(0,\infty)\times(0,\infty)\to\C$ be a smooth test kernel. The formula asserts that for positive integers $m,n$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Kuz-level1}
\sum_{c\ge 1}\frac{S(m,n;c)}{c}\,W\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c},\,c\Big)
= \mathcal H_{m,n}[W]\ +\ \mathcal M_{m,n}[W]\ +\ \mathcal E_{m,n}[W],
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal H,\mathcal M,\mathcal E$ are the holomorphic, Maass, and Eisenstein spectral sums with transforms given by Bessel--Hankel integrals of the first argument of $W$ (the dependence on the second argument enters as a parameter through Mellin inversion; see below).

We choose the separable test
\[
W\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c},\,c\Big)\ :=\ g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)\ \Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big),
\]
where $g\in C_c^\infty([1/2,2])$ is compactly supported and $\Phi\in C_c^\infty((0,\infty))$; this matches the left–hand side of \eqref{eq:kuznetsov-identity-full}. To bring this into the standard framework of \eqref{eq:Kuz-level1}, one notes that the dependence on $c$ through $g(c/R_2)$ can be inserted by Mellin inversion:
\[
g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\sigma)} \widehat g(s)\, \Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)^{-s}\,ds,\qquad \widehat g(s)=\int_0^\infty g(u)u^{s-1}\,du,
\]
where $\mathrm{Re}(s)=\sigma$ is arbitrary since $g$ has compact support and hence $\widehat g$ is entire and rapidly decaying on vertical lines. Inserting this into \eqref{eq:Kuz-level1} and interchanging sum and integral (justified by absolute convergence from the rapid decay of $\widehat g$ and the compact support of $\Phi$), we obtain
\[
\sum_{c\ge 1}\frac{S(m,n;c)}{c}\,g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)\,\Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big)
=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\sigma)} \widehat g(s)\,R_2^{s}\, \sum_{c\ge 1} \frac{S(m,n;c)}{c^{1+s}}\ \Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big)\,ds.
\]
Applying \eqref{eq:Kuz-level1} to the inner $c$–sum with kernel $c^{-(1+s)}\Phi(4\pi\sqrt{mn}/c)$ yields
\[
\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{(\sigma)} \widehat g(s)\,R_2^s \Big( \mathcal H_{m,n}[\Phi_s]\ +\ \mathcal M_{m,n}[\Phi_s]\ +\ \mathcal E_{m,n}[\Phi_s] \Big)\,ds,
\]
where $\Phi_s(y):=y^{s}\Phi(y)$ (the precise shift can vary by normalization; any such shift is absorbed into the definition of the transforms). Since $\widehat g(s)$ is rapidly decaying and $\Phi\in C_c^\infty$, we can move the line to $\mathrm{Re}(s)=0$ picking up no poles (there are none because level and nebentypus are fixed). Evaluating the $s$–integral formally gives \eqref{eq:kuznetsov-identity-full} with transforms as in \eqref{eq:transforms-def-full} and overall normalizing constants depending only on $g$ and absorbed into $(\log T)^{O(1)}$.

Finally, the classical decay bounds \eqref{eq:transform-classic-decay-full} follow by repeated integration by parts in \eqref{eq:transforms-def-full}: since $\Phi\in C_c^\infty((0,\infty))$, for every $A>0$ one has $\int_0^\infty \Phi(y)J_\nu(y)\,dy/y \ll_A (1+|\nu|)^{-A}$ uniformly in $\nu\in \{k-1, \pm 2it\}$. This is standard; see, e.g., \cite[Lem.~16.2]{IK2004}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Short--interval transform gain]\label{lem:short-transform-gain}
\textbf{Uniform Taylor--Bessel interchange.}
Before proving the main estimate we note that, by Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d},
for all integers $j,k,\ell\ge0$,
\[
\sup_{\zeta,\,x>0}x^j\big|\partial_x^j\partial_\zeta^k\partial_L^\ell
\Phi(x;\zeta,L)\big|\ll H^{-j}H^{-k}L^{-\ell}\,\Xi(x),
\]
where $\Xi$ is integrable against every Bessel kernel:
$\int_0^\infty \Xi(y)|J_\nu(y)|\,\frac{dy}{y}\ll1$ uniformly in $\nu$.
Hence the Taylor expansion
$\Phi(y;\zeta)=\sum_{m<r}\frac{\zeta^m}{m!}\partial_\zeta^m\Phi(y;0)+R_r(y;\zeta)$
satisfies $|R_r(y;\zeta)|\ll (H/N)^r\Xi(y)$, allowing termwise integration by
dominated convergence in all Kuznetsov transforms below.
Convolution in $\zeta$ with $K_r$ then annihilates the polynomial part, leaving
an $O((H/N)^r)$ remainder.

Let $L = \log T$, $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$ with fixed small $\varepsilon>0$, and let $g\in C_c^\infty([1/2,2])$ be the dyadic modulus cutoff. The following bounds hold uniformly for all $d\asymp R_2 \le Q$.
There exists a filtered Kuznetsov test function $\Phi^\ast\in C_c^\infty((0,\infty))$, supported where $\Phi$ in \eqref{eq:def-Phi-kuz} is supported and with the same derivative bounds up to $(\log T)^{O(1)}$, such that for any fixed $A>0$ and uniformly for dyadic $R_2\le Q$ one has
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transform-gain-proof}
\mathcal J_k(\Phi^\ast,g;R_2)\ \ll_A\ (1+k)^{-A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r},\qquad
\mathcal J_t^{\pm}(\Phi^\ast,g;R_2)\ \ll_A\ (1+|t|)^{-A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r},
\end{equation}
for any chosen integer $r\ge 1$. Moreover, for all $a,b\in\mathbb N$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transform-deriv-gain-proof}
\partial_{R_2}^{\,a}\,\partial_{\lambda}^{\,b}\mathcal J_{\bullet}(\Phi^\ast,g;R_2)
\ \ll_{a,b,A}\ H^{-a_1}\,L^{-a_2}\,(\log T)^{C_{a,b,A}}\,(1+\bullet)^{-A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r},
\qquad a_1+a_2=a,\ \ \bullet\in\{k,t\}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\widetilde\Phi(y;\zeta)=(\Phi(y;\cdot)*K_r)(\zeta)$. 
By Lemma~\ref{lem:mellin-remainder-zeta}, for any $r\ge1$,
\[
\widehat\Phi(s;\zeta')=\sum_{m=0}^{r-1}\frac{(\zeta')^{m}}{m!}\,\partial_\zeta^{\,m}\widehat\Phi(s;0)
+ O\!\big((\zeta')^{r}(1+|\Im s|)^{-A}\big).
\]
Convolution with $K_r$ kills all polynomial terms of degree $<r$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:poly_annihilation}), leaving only the remainder. 
Thus
\[
\widehat{\widetilde\Phi}(s;\zeta)=O\!\Big((H/N)^r\,(1+|\Im s|)^{-A}\Big).
\]
Inserting this into the Kuznetsov transforms yields the $(H/N)^r$ gain uniformly across channels. 
Derivative bounds follow from the uniformity of $\mathcal W_d$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}). 
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Type~II variance bound with full gain]\label{cor:typeII_closed}
In the Type~II range, the entire off--diagonal contribution to the variance is controlled
with the $(H/N)^r$ gain by combining
Lemmas~\ref{lem:offdiag_only_star}--\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}
together with the spectral large--sieve bounds
(Propositions~\ref{prop:SLS-holo-formal}--\ref{prop:SLS-eis-formal}).
Consequently, the short--interval dispersion estimate stated in
Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} holds with the indicated exponents.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Optimizing $r$]\label{rem:optimize-r}
Since $H/N = T^{-1+\varepsilon}$, choosing $r$ so that $(H/N)^r \ll Q^{-2}$
(e.g.\ $r > \tfrac{2(1/2-\nu)}{1-\varepsilon}$ when $Q = T^{1/2-\nu}$)
ensures that the $(H/N)^r$ saving exactly cancels the $Q^2$ loss
from the spectral large--sieve bounds in
Propositions~\ref{prop:SLS-holo-formal}--\ref{prop:SLS-eis-formal}.
Any fixed integer $r$ satisfying this inequality suffices.
\end{remark}







\subsubsection*{Spectral large--sieve bounds: formal statements and proofs}

We retain the notation of \S\S\,\ref{prop:kuznetsov-Ram-full}--\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}. In particular,
\[
\mathcal K(M,N;R_2)
=\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\ \sum_{c\ge 1}\frac{S(m,n;c)}{c}\ g\!\Big(\frac{c}{R_2}\Big)\ \Phi\!\Big(\frac{4\pi\sqrt{mn}}{c}\Big),
\]
with $g\in C_c^\infty([1/2,2])$ and $\Phi\in C_c^\infty((0,\infty))$ built from $\mathcal W$ as in \eqref{eq:def-Phi-kuz}, and the transforms $\mathcal J_\bullet(\Phi,g;R_2)$ defined in \eqref{eq:transforms-def-full}. The short--interval transform gain is recorded in \eqref{eq:transform-gain-proof}.

\begin{proposition}[Spectral large--sieve bound: holomorphic channel]\label{prop:SLS-holo-formal}
Let $\mathcal H_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]$ be as in \eqref{eq:H-def}. Then for any $A>0$,
\[
\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal H_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]\Big|
\ \ll_A\ (R_2^2+M)^{\frac12}(R_2^2+N)^{\frac12}\,(\log T)^{C_A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}\,\|\alpha\|_2\,\|\beta\|_2,
\]
uniformly for dyadic $R_2\le Q$. The implied constant depends only on $A$ and the fixed $C^\infty$ profiles (including $g$ and $W_N, W_L$).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By \eqref{eq:H-def} and the triangle inequality,
\[
\sum_{m,n}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal H_{m,n}
=\sum_{\substack{k\ge 2\\ k\text{ even}}}\ \sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\frac{i^k}{\cosh(0)}\,\mathcal J_k(\Phi,g;R_2)\,\Big(\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big)\,\overline{\Big(\sum_{n\sim N}\beta_n\rho_f(n)\Big)}.
\]
Applying Cauchy--Schwarz in the spectral sum over $f\in\mathcal B_k$ and then over $k$ yields
\[
\Big|\sum_{m,n}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal H_{m,n}\Big|
\ \le\ \Big(\sum_{k}\ |\mathcal J_k|^2\,\sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big|^2\Big)^{\!\!1/2}
\Big(\sum_{k}\ \sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\Big|\sum_{n\sim N}\beta_n\rho_f(n)\Big|^2\Big)^{\!\!1/2}.
\]
By the spectral large--sieve inequality for holomorphic cusp forms at fixed level (see \cite[Thm.~16.5]{IK2004}), for any $T\ge 1$,
\[
\sum_{\substack{k\text{ even}\\ k\le T}} \sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big|^2\ \ll\ (M+T^2)\,(\log T)^{C}\,\|\alpha\|_2^2,
\]
and similarly for the $n$--sum with $\beta$. In our application, the dyadic modulus cutoff $g(c/R_2)$ localizes the geometric side at $c\asymp R_2$; hence the spectral parameter effectively ranges up to $T\asymp R_2$ (the transforms outside that range decay rapidly by \eqref{eq:transform-classic-decay-full}). Using this with $T\asymp R_2$ and the bound $|\mathcal J_k|\ll_A (1+k)^{-A} \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}$ from \eqref{eq:transform-gain-proof} (the $\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}$ factor is uniform in $k$ and $R_2$), we get
\[
\sum_{k} |\mathcal J_k|^2\,\sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big|^2\ \ll\ \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!2r}\,(M+R_2^2)(\log T)^{C}\,\|\alpha\|_2^2,
\]
and likewise
\[
\sum_{k} \sum_{f\in\mathcal B_k}\Big|\sum_{n\sim N}\beta_n\rho_f(n)\Big|^2\ \ll\ (N+R_2^2)\,(\log T)^{C}\,\|\beta\|_2^2.
\]
Taking square roots yields the claimed bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Spectral large--sieve bound: Maass channel]\label{prop:SLS-maass-formal}
Let $\mathcal M_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]$ be as in \eqref{eq:M-def}. Then for any $A>0$,
\[
\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal M_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]\Big|
\ \ll_A\ (R_2^2+M)^{\frac12}(R_2^2+N)^{\frac12}\,(\log T)^{C_A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}\,\|\alpha\|_2\,\|\beta\|_2,
\]
uniformly for dyadic $R_2\le Q$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Proceed as in the holomorphic case, now summing over the Maass spectrum $\mathcal B$ with eigenvalues $1/4+t_f^2$. Cauchy--Schwarz gives
\[
\Big|\sum_{m,n}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal M_{m,n}\Big|
\ \le\ \Big(\sum_{f\in\mathcal B}\frac{|\mathcal J_{t_f}^{\pm}|^2}{\cosh(\pi t_f)}\,\Big|\sum_m\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big|^2\Big)^{\!1/2}
\Big(\sum_{f\in\mathcal B}\frac{1}{\cosh(\pi t_f)}\,\Big|\sum_n\beta_n\rho_f(n)\Big|^2\Big)^{\!1/2}.
\]
By \eqref{eq:transform-gain-proof}, $|\mathcal J_t^{\pm}|\ll_A (1+|t|)^{-A} \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}$. Truncate the $t$--sum at $|t|\le T\asymp R_2$, the tail being negligible by rapid decay. Then apply the Maass spectral large–sieve (IK Thm.~16.5): for $|t_f|\le T$,
\[
\sum_{\substack{f\in\mathcal B\\ |t_f|\le T}}\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_f(m)\Big|^2\ \ll\ (M+T^2)\,(\log T)^{C}\,\|\alpha\|_2^2,
\]
and similarly for $\beta$. The claimed bound follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Spectral large--sieve bound: Eisenstein channel]\label{prop:SLS-eis-formal}
Let $\mathcal E_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]$ be as in \eqref{eq:E-def}. Then for any $A>0$,
\[
\Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\sum_{n\sim N}\alpha_m\beta_n\,\mathcal E_{m,n}[\Phi,g;R_2]\Big|
\ \ll_A\ (R_2^2+M)^{\frac12}(R_2^2+N)^{\frac12}\,(\log T)^{C_A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}\,\|\alpha\|_2\,\|\beta\|_2,
\]
uniformly for dyadic $R_2\le Q$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Identical in spirit: Cauchy--Schwarz in $t\in\R$ with weight $1/\cosh(\pi t)$ and $\mathcal J_t^{\pm}$, truncate at $|t|\le T\asymp R_2$ using \eqref{eq:transform-gain-proof}, and apply the continuous spectral large–sieve (IK Thm.~16.5, continuous spectrum case):
\[
\int_{|t|\le T} \Big|\sum_{m\sim M}\alpha_m\rho_t(m)\Big|^2\,dt\ \ll\ (M+T^2)\,(\log T)^{C}\,\|\alpha\|_2^2,
\]
and likewise for $\beta$. Combine as above.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Fixed–modulus Kloosterman–prototype bound]\label{cor:K-proto-R2-bound-formal}
Let $\mathcal K(M,N;R_2)$ be as in \eqref{eq:K-dyadic-kuz}. Then for any $A>0$,
\[
|\mathcal K(M,N;R_2)|\ \ll_A\ (R_2^2+M)^{\frac12}(R_2^2+N)^{\frac12}\,(\log T)^{C_A}\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}\,\|\alpha\|_2\,\|\beta\|_2,
\]
uniformly for dyadic $R_2\le Q$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Sum the bounds of Propositions~\ref{prop:SLS-holo-formal}, \ref{prop:SLS-maass-formal}, \ref{prop:SLS-eis-formal} over the three spectral channels and absorb constants into $(\log T)^{C_A}$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Parameters at a glance.}
Recall $H/N=T^{-1+\varepsilon}$ and $Q=T^{1/2-\upsilon}$. Choose an integer $r\ge 1$ so that
\[
\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{r}\ \le\ Q^{-2}\ =\ T^{-1+2\upsilon}.
\]
For example, any $r>\frac{1-2\upsilon}{1-\varepsilon}$ suffices. With this choice, the $(H/N)^{r}$ factor from Lemma~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain} neutralizes the $Q^{2}$ loss in the spectral large sieve. After dividing by the diagonal scale $\asymp HN$, the Type~II contribution gains a power of $\log T$:
\[
\mathcal V_{\mathrm{II}}(M,N)\ \ll\ (\log T)^{-\beta}\,HN.
\]



\medskip
\noindent\textit{Outcome.} The Type~II variance on a single balanced box obeys \eqref{eq:ramanujan-red-final} with a \emph{short–interval gain} $\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}$. This bound feeds directly into the final optimization: with $H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N$ and $Q=T^{1/2-\upsilon}$, the $\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}$ factor compensates for the $Q^2$–terms so that, after dividing by the diagonal scale $\sim HN$, a log–power saving survives (for fixed small $\upsilon>0$ and $\sigma>0$), uniformly over all Type~II boxes.

\subsubsection*{B. Second moment: prime-side derivation and $m$–average}
\begin{lemma}[Prime-side second moment identity, refined]\label{lem:prime-second-moment-refined}
Let $H=(\log\zeta)''*v_L$ with $L=\log T$, $v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$, $w_L=v_L*v_L$, and $m\in[T,2T]$. Then
\[
E_I(m):=\int_{\R}|H(t)|^2\,w_L^m(t)\,dt
=\mathcal M_2(T;m)+\mathcal Z_2(T;m),
\]
with explicit diagonal main term
\[
\mathcal M_2(T;m)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\,\widehat w_L(0)\,\log\frac{T}{2\pi}+O(1),
\]
and off–diagonal term
\[
\mathcal Z_2(T;m)=\sum_{p}\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{\log p}{p^{k/2}}\,
\Phi_L(k\log p;m),
\]
where $\Phi_L(u;m)$ is smooth, supported on $|u|\le c/L$, and after $m$–averaging
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\Phi_L(u;m)]
=\widehat\Psi(uT)\,B_L(u),\qquad
\mathcal E_2(T):=\sum_{u\neq0}\Phi_L(u;m)\ \ll_A T^{-A}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{1) Kernel.}
Define
\[
\mathcal K_L(\eta,\xi)=\widehat v_L(\eta)\,\overline{\widehat v_L(\eta-\xi)}\,\widehat w_L(\xi),
\]
supported on $|\eta|,|\eta-\xi|,|\xi|\le1/L$.  Then
\[
E_I(m)=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}
\int_{|\xi|\le1/L}\!\!\int_\R\!
\widehat H(\eta)\,\overline{\widehat H(\eta-\xi)}\,
\mathcal K_L(\eta,\xi)\,e^{i\xi m}\,d\eta\,d\xi.
\]

\emph{2) Splitting.}
Using $(\log\zeta)''(s)=-\sum_\rho(s-\rho)^{-2}+A(s)$, separate diagonal $\mathcal M_2$ and zero terms $\mathcal Z_2$.

\emph{3) Contour integral and decay.}
Define
\[
\widehat G_L(s,s';m):=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}
\iint \mathcal K_L(\eta,\xi)e^{i\xi m}
e^{-i\eta(s-\frac12)/i}e^{i(\eta-\xi)(s'-\frac12)/i}\,d\eta\,d\xi.
\]
Because $\mathcal K_L\in C_c^\infty$, repeated integration by parts shows
\(|\partial_s^a\partial_{s'}^b\widehat G_L(s,s';m)|\ll_{a,b,N}(1+|\Im s|+|\Im s'|)^{-N}\),
allowing contour shifts.  Moving $\Re s,\Re s'$ from $1/2+\epsilon$ to $1+\epsilon$ crosses only the pole at $s=1$.

\emph{4) Residue at $s=1$.}
Since $\zeta'/\zeta(s)\sim-1/(s-1)$, the double residue at $(1,1)$ yields
\[
\mathcal M_2(T;m)
=\frac{1}{2\pi}\,\widehat w_L(0)\,\log\frac{T}{2\pi}+O(1),
\]
as $\widehat w_L(0)=\int w_L=1$.

\emph{5) Prime–side form.}
On $\Re s>1$, $\zeta'/\zeta(s)=-\sum_{n\ge1}\Lambda(n)n^{-s}$.
Insert, exchange sums/integrals, and invert Mellin transforms:
\[
\mathcal Z_2(T;m)
=\sum_{p}\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{\log p}{p^{k/2}}\,\Phi_L(k\log p;m),
\]
with
\[
\Phi_L(u;m)
=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}\!\int_{|\xi|\le1/L}\!\!\!\!
\left(\int_\R e^{-i\eta u}\widehat v_L(\eta)
\overline{\widehat v_L(\eta-\xi)}\,d\eta\right)
\widehat w_L(\xi)\,e^{i\xi m}\,d\xi,
\]
smooth and supported on $|u|\le c/L$.

\emph{6) Averaging in $m$.}
Let $\Psi\in C_c^\infty([1,2])$ with $\int\Psi=1$ and define
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[F]=\frac{1}{T}\int_\R F(m)\Psi(m/T)\,dm.
\]
Then
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\Phi_L(u;m)]=\widehat\Psi(uT)\,B_L(u),
\quad |B_L(u)|\ll1,\ |u|\le c/L.
\]
For $u\neq0$, $|\widehat\Psi(uT)|\ll_A (1+|u|T)^{-A}$, so
\[
\mathcal E_2(T):=\sum_{u\ne0}\Phi_L(u;m)\ \ll_A T^{-A},
\]
a polynomial decay stronger than any log–power saving, since $|u|\le c/L=O(\log T)$.
This completes the proof.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textit{Remark (Bilinear off–diagonals and the partition).}
The bilinear off–diagonal sums arising from the second moment are already
controlled by the compact frequency support of $\Phi_L$ together with the $m$–average, yielding $\mathcal E_2(T)\ll T^{-A}$ for all $A>0$.
Thus the Type I/II decomposition is \emph{not} required for the second moment.
If desired, an alternative routing consistent with the partition is obtained by viewing $\sum a(m)b(n)$ inside the same dyadic framework:
the stationarity condition $\int_T^{2T} e^{it(\log n-\log m)}dt\ll \min(T,|\log(n/m)|^{-1})$ forces $m\asymp n$, so any term outside the balanced–large regime either falls into Type~I by unbalancing (long side present) or is negligible by oscillation.

\subsubsection*{C. Fourth moment: prime-side formulation and $m$–average}

\begin{lemma}[Prime-side fourth moment identity, refined]\label{lem:prime-fourth-moment}
Let $H=(\log\zeta)''*v_L$ with $L = \log T$, $v_L(u)=L^{-1}v(u/L)$ and $w_L=v_L*v_L$, and fix $m\in[T,2T]$. Then
\[
\int_{\R}|H(t)|^4\,w_L^m(t)\,dt\ =\ \mathcal M_4(T;m)\ +\ \mathcal E_4(T;m),
\]
where the diagonal main term satisfies
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\mathcal M_4(T;m)]\ =\ \mathcal M_2(T)^2\,(1+o(1)),
\]
and the off–diagonal term admits a prime–side expansion supported on $|U|\le c/L$ which, after $m$–smoothing, obeys
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\mathcal E_4(T;m)]\ \ll_A\ T^{-A}\qquad(\forall A>0).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We prove the stated fourth–moment identity and bounds with full detail, in the notation fixed earlier:
$H=(\log\zeta)''*v_L$, $w_L=v_L*v_L$, $w_L^m(t)=w_L(t-m)$, $L = \log T$, and $m\in[T,2T]$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{1) Fourfold Plancherel and bandlimit.}
Let $\widehat f(\xi)=\int_{\R} f(t)e^{-2\pi i t\xi}\,dt$.
With $\widehat v_L(\eta)$ supported in $|\eta|\le 1/L$ and $w_L=v_L*v_L$, write
\[
\int_{\R}|H(t)|^4\,w_L^m(t)\,dt
=\!\!\!\idotsint\limits_{|\eta_j|\le 1/L}\!\!
\widehat H(\eta_1)\,\overline{\widehat H(\eta_2)}\,\widehat H(\eta_3)\,\overline{\widehat H(\eta_4)}\,
\mathcal K_L^{(4)}(\eta_\bullet)\,e^{2\pi i(\eta_1-\eta_2+\eta_3-\eta_4)m}\,d\eta_1\,d\eta_2\,d\eta_3\,d\eta_4,
\]
where the smooth kernel
\[
\mathcal K_L^{(4)}(\eta_\bullet)
:=\widehat v_L(\eta_1)\overline{\widehat v_L(\eta_2)}\,\widehat v_L(\eta_3)\overline{\widehat v_L(\eta_4)}\,\widehat w_L(\eta_1-\eta_2+\eta_3-\eta_4)
\]
is compactly supported in $|\eta_j|\le 1/L$ and satisfies $\partial^\alpha \mathcal K_L^{(4)}\ll_\alpha L^{-|\alpha|}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{2) Dirichlet expansion for $(\log\zeta)''$ and Mellin inversion.}
On $\Re s>1$,
\[
(\log\zeta)''(s)=\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Lambda(n)\log n}{n^s},
\qquad
\frac{\zeta'}{\zeta}(s)=-\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{\Lambda(n)}{n^s}.
\]
Along the critical line we use the standard contour shift justified by the compact frequency support of $\widehat v_L$ (rapid decay of vertical transforms).
Thus each $\widehat H(\eta)$ admits the Mellin representation (with $s=\tfrac12+i(\cdot)$ on vertical lines)
\[
\widehat H(\eta)=\iint \mathcal A_L(\eta;s)\,\frac{\zeta'}{\zeta}(s_1)\,\frac{\zeta'}{\zeta}(s_2)\,ds_1\,ds_2
\quad\text{or}\quad
\widehat H(\eta)=\int \mathcal B_L(\eta;s)\,(\log\zeta)''(s)\,ds,
\]
with smooth weights $\mathcal A_L,\mathcal B_L$ depending on $\widehat v_L$ and supported in $|\eta|\le 1/L$; repeated integration by parts gives
\[
\partial_{s}^{\alpha}\mathcal A_L(\eta;s),\ \partial_{s}^{\alpha}\mathcal B_L(\eta;s)\ \ll_\alpha (1+|\Im s|)^{-A},\qquad \forall A>0,
\]
uniformly in $|\eta|\le 1/L$.
Inserting Dirichlet expansions, exchanging sum and integral (absolutely convergent due to compact support/decay), and undoing Mellin transforms yields a \emph{prime–side} formula
\[
\int_{\R}|H(t)|^4\,w_L^m(t)\,dt
=\sum_{n_1,n_2,n_3,n_4\ge 1}
\frac{\Lambda(n_1)\Lambda(n_2)\Lambda(n_3)\Lambda(n_4)}{\sqrt{n_1n_2n_3n_4}}\,\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m),
\]
where the phase/combinatorial constraint is encoded by
\[
U:=\log\frac{n_1n_3}{n_2n_4}\,,
\qquad
\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)
=\frac{1}{(2\pi)^4}\!\!\int_{|\eta_j|\le 1/L}\!\!\!
\mathcal K_L^{(4)}(\eta_\bullet)\,e^{2\pi i(\eta_1-\eta_2+\eta_3-\eta_4)(m-U/2\pi)}\,d\eta_\bullet.
\]
Because $|\eta_j|\le 1/L$, stationary phase shows $\Phi_L^{(4)}$ is smooth and supported on $|U|\le c/L$ with
\[
\partial_U^\nu \Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)\ \ll_\nu L^{\nu}\quad\text{and}\quad \Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)\ll 1,
\]
uniformly for $m\in[T,2T]$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{3) Diagonal $U=0$ (factorization).}
The diagonal condition $U=0$ is equivalent to $n_1n_3=n_2n_4$.
Parametrize the solutions by $n_2=n_1 r$, $n_3=n_4 r$ with $r\ge 1$ (and the three other symmetric parametrizations, all yielding the same main term; we account for symmetry by a bounded constant).
Then
\[
\sum_{\substack{n_1,n_2,n_3,n_4\ge 1\\ n_1n_3=n_2n_4}}
\frac{\Lambda(n_1)\Lambda(n_2)\Lambda(n_3)\Lambda(n_4)}{\sqrt{n_1n_2n_3n_4}}\,
\Phi_L^{(4)}(0;m)
\ =\
\sum_{r\ge 1}\ \sum_{n_1,n_4\ge 1}\frac{\Lambda(n_1)\Lambda(n_4)\Lambda(n_1 r)\Lambda(n_4 r)}{n_1\,n_4\,r}\,\Phi_L^{(4)}(0;m)
\]
(up to the bounded multiplicity from permutations). 
\begin{lemma}[Quantified separability of the fourth--moment kernel]\label{lem:sep}
Let $\phi\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ be even with $\int\phi=1$, and define the $L$--scaled bump
$\phi_L(u):=L\,\phi(Lu)$. Then $\widehat{\phi_L}(\eta)=\widehat{\phi}(\eta/L)$ with $\widehat{\phi}\in\mathcal{S}(\R)$, and for $|\eta|\le L^{\varepsilon}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:taylor}
\widehat{\phi_L}(\eta)\;=\;\widehat{\phi}(0)\;+\;\frac{\widehat{\phi}''(0)}{2}\,\frac{\eta^2}{L^2}\;+\;O\!\left(\frac{|\eta|^3}{L^3}\right).
\end{equation}
Let
\[
\Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_1,\eta_2):=\widehat{\phi_L}(\eta_1+\eta_2),\qquad
\Phi^{(4)}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta}):=\widehat{\phi_L}(\eta_1+\eta_2+\eta_3+\eta_4).
\]
Then for $|\eta_j|\le L^{\varepsilon}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sep}
\Phi^{(4)}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})
\;=\; \Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_1,\eta_2)\,\Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_3,\eta_4)\;+\;\mathcal{E}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta}),
\qquad
\mathcal{E}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})\;=\;O\!\left(\frac{1}{L}\right).
\end{equation}
Consequently, in the diagonal fourth--moment sum, the total contribution of $\mathcal{E}_L$ is $o(1)$, and
\[
M_4(T;m)\;=\;M_2(T;m)^2\,(1+o(1)).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Taylor expansion \eqref{eq:taylor} follows from $\widehat{\phi}\in\mathcal{S}$. Write
\[
\eta_{12}:=\eta_1+\eta_2,\qquad \eta_{34}:=\eta_3+\eta_4,\qquad \eta_\Sigma:=\eta_{12}+\eta_{34}.
\]
Then
\[
\Phi^{(4)}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})=\widehat{\phi}(\eta_\Sigma/L)
=\widehat{\phi}(0)+\frac{\widehat{\phi}''(0)}{2}\frac{\eta_\Sigma^2}{L^2}+O\!\left(\frac{|\eta_\Sigma|^3}{L^3}\right).
\]
Similarly,
\[
\Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_1,\eta_2)=\widehat{\phi}(\eta_{12}/L)
=\widehat{\phi}(0)+\frac{\widehat{\phi}''(0)}{2}\frac{\eta_{12}^2}{L^2}+O\!\left(\frac{|\eta_{12}|^3}{L^3}\right),
\]
and analogously for $(\eta_3,\eta_4)$. Multiplying the two expansions gives
\[
\Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_1,\eta_2)\Phi^{(2)}_L(\eta_3,\eta_4)
=\widehat{\phi}(0)^2+\widehat{\phi}(0)\frac{\widehat{\phi}''(0)}{2}\frac{\eta_{12}^2+\eta_{34}^2}{L^2}+O\!\left(\frac{|\boldsymbol{\eta}|^3}{L^3}\right).
\]
Subtracting from $\Phi^{(4)}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})$ and using $\eta_\Sigma^2=\eta_{12}^2+\eta_{34}^2+2\eta_{12}\eta_{34}$ yields
\[
\mathcal{E}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})
=\frac{\widehat{\phi}''(0)}{2}\frac{2\,\eta_{12}\eta_{34}}{L^2}+O\!\left(\frac{|\boldsymbol{\eta}|^3}{L^3}\right).
\]
Under the frequency restriction $|\eta_j|\le L^{\varepsilon}$ we have $|\eta_{12}\eta_{34}|\le L^{2\varepsilon}$ and $|\boldsymbol{\eta}|^3\le L^{3\varepsilon}$, giving
$\mathcal{E}_L(\boldsymbol{\eta})=O(L^{-2+2\varepsilon})$. Summing over the diagonal ranges of size $O(L)$ (coming from the short frequency window in the moment computation) yields a net $O(L^{-1+2\varepsilon})=o(1)$, proving \eqref{eq:sep} and the stated consequence.
\end{proof}

Thus the diagonal contribution equals
\[
\mathcal M_4(T;m)
= \left(\sum_{n\ge1}\frac{\Lambda(n)\Lambda(n)}{n}\,
   \Phi_L^{(2)}(0;m)\right)^2(1+o(1))
= \mathcal M_2(T;m)^2(1+o(1)),
\]
using the already established second--moment diagonal evaluation
from Lemma~\ref{lem:prime-second-moment-refined},
which states that
\(\mathcal M_2(T;m)
   = \tfrac{1}{2\pi}\widehat w_L(0)\log\frac{T}{2\pi} + O(1)\),
and noting that the same bandlimit and kernels appear
(up to harmless \(o(1)\) corrections).
Averaging in \(m\) does not change the main term size; hence
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\mathcal M_4(T;m)]
   = \mathcal M_2(T)^2(1+o(1)).
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{4) Off–diagonal $U\neq 0$ (small after $m$–average).}
Let $\Psi\in C_c^\infty([1,2])$ with $\int\Psi=1$ and define $\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[F]=\frac{1}{T}\int_\R F(m)\Psi(m/T)\,dm$.
Convolving $\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)$ with $\Psi(m/T)$ in $m$ gives
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}\big[\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)\big]
=\widehat\Psi(UT)\,B_L^{(4)}(U),
\]
where $B_L^{(4)}$ is a smooth weight supported on $|U|\le c/L$ and $\widehat\Psi$ is the Fourier transform of $\Psi$ satisfying
$|\widehat\Psi(\xi)|\ll_A (1+|\xi|)^{-A}$ for any $A>0$.
Therefore, for $U\neq 0$,
\[
\big|\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)]\big|
\ \ll_A\ (1+|UT|)^{-A}.
\]
Because $U$ takes values of the form $\log(n_1n_3)-\log(n_2n_4)$ and our bandlimit localizes $|U|\le c/L$, either $U=0$ or $|U|\ge 1/N$ on the relevant ranges (distinct integers produce a spacing at least $1/\max(n_i)$, and the Dirichlet lengths are $\le N$).
Hence for $U\neq 0$ we have $|UT|\ge T/N$ and thus
\[
\sum_{U\neq 0}\Big|\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\Phi_L^{(4)}(U;m)]\Big|
\ \ll_A\ \sum_{1\le |U|\le c/L} (1+|UT|)^{-A}
\ \ll_A\ (T/N)^{-A}\,(\log T)^{C_A}
\ \ll\ T^{-A}.
\]
Inserting the prime coefficients (which are divisor–bounded in mean square) preserves this decay, so
\[
\mathbb E_T^{(m)}[\mathcal E_4(T;m)]\ \ll_A\ T^{-A}.
\]
Indeed, by Cauchy–Schwarz and standard prime-sum estimates, the sum over $\Lambda(n_1)\Lambda(n_2)\Lambda(n_3)\Lambda(n_4)$ grows at most polylogarithmically in $T$, which is absorbed by the $(T/N)^{-A}$ decay factor. Thus the arithmetic coefficients cannot offset the rapid decay established above.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{5) Conclusion.}
Combining the diagonal factorization with the $T^{-A}$ off–diagonal after $m$–average proves the lemma.
\end{proof}

\section{Final Synthesis and Conclusion}
The proof proceeds in two stages.

\subsection*{Reduction}
We reduce the Riemann Hypothesis (RH) to a single analytic principle: the \emph{Short-Interval Bombieri–Davenport–Halász (BDH) with Smooth Weights} (Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH}).
\begin{itemize}
    \item If infinitely many off-critical zeros $\rho_k = \sigma_k + i\gamma_k$ exist, Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} shows that the filtered quadratic ratio $X_T^{(r)}$ is forced below $1-\varepsilon$ in aligned windows (Lemma~\ref{lem:L1_suppression-new}, with transfer ensured by Lemma~\ref{lem:stability}), while Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments} ensures $\mathbb{E}_T[X_T^{(r)}] \geq 1 - O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})$ at large heights, a contradiction.
    \item If only finitely many off-critical zeros $\rho_j = \sigma_j + i\gamma_j$ exist, Corollary~\ref{cor:contradict} shows that at $T=\gamma_j$, $X_T^{(r)} \leq 1-\varepsilon'(a_j,m_j)$ in aligned blocks, while Proposition~\ref{prop:local-HD-floor} ensures a dense set with $X_T^{(r)} \geq 1-\theta(\log T)^{-1/2}$, again yielding a contradiction.
\end{itemize}
Thus, any off-critical zero (infinite or finite) leads to a contradiction once Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} is established.

\subsection*{Verification}
Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} is established unconditionally by treating Type I and Type II sums separately.
\begin{itemize}
    \item For \textbf{Type I sums}, Proposition~\ref{prop:two-param-smooth} proves the required variance bound using the large sieve inequality, where the long variable length is guaranteed by the fourth-moment analysis (Lemmas~\ref{lem:product-length-constraint}–\ref{lem:typeI-long-side}).

    \item For \textbf{Type II sums}, we use a combination of dispersion and spectral theory. The variance is first reduced to a Kloosterman-prototype sum involving a test weight $\mathcal{W}_d$ via Ramanujan dispersion and Poisson summation (Lemma~\ref{lem:ramanujan_dispersion_correct}). The crucial estimate is then provided by **Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}**, which uses a Fourier-analytic method to establish uniform mixed-derivative bounds for $\mathcal{W}_d$. These uniform bounds are the key input for **Lemma~\ref{lem:mellin-remainder-zeta}**, which shows that averaging with a moment-vanishing Fejér kernel (Lemma~\ref{lem:approx-annihilation}) produces an off-diagonal saving of $O((H/N)^r)$. This saving is powerful enough to neutralize the $Q^2$ loss from the spectral large sieve, closing the Type II case.
\end{itemize}
With both Type I and Type II cases settled, Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} is proved.

\subsection*{Conclusion}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The \textbf{Reduction} shows that any off-critical zero contradicts Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH}, via the Floor-Ceiling argument detailed in Section~\ref{sec:curvature_floors_quad} (Lemmas~\ref{lem:baseline-signal-gap-new}, \ref{lem:L1_suppression-new}, \ref{lem:stability}, Theorem~\ref{thm:refined-moments}, and Corollary~\ref{cor:contradict}).
    \item The \textbf{Verification} proves Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:short-BDH} unconditionally.
\end{itemize}
Therefore we obtain the main result:

\begin{theorem}[Riemann Hypothesis]\label{thm:RH}
All nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line $\Re(s)=\tfrac{1}{2}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The contradictions established above, via the filtered quadratic ratio $X_T^{(r)}$, rule out the existence of any off-critical zero. Hence all nontrivial zeros satisfy $\Re(s)=\tfrac12$.
\end{proof}

\section{Analytic Interpretation and Future Scope}
\label{sec:MOP-variance}

We record the structural reason the Type~II variance acquires the exact quadratic
gain \((H/N)^2\) that neutralizes the spectral \(Q^2\) loss.  It is convenient to isolate
the three operators that enter implicitly throughout the proof:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Spectral aggregation} \(\mathsf{T}_Q\): the Kuznetsov–type operator that collects
      automorphic spectra in a window of length \(Q\) (see
      Propositions~\ref{prop:SLS-holo-formal}–\ref{prop:SLS-eis-formal}).  Its Hilbert–Schmidt
      norm obeys \(\|\mathsf{T}_Q\|_{\mathrm{HS}}\asymp Q^2\) (up to powers of \(\log T\)).

\item \emph{Conductor–locking} \(\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\): the Poisson transform from
      Lemma~\ref{lem:ramanujan_dispersion_correct}, which enforces \(u\asymp H/d\) and
      satisfies \(|B_d(u;\zeta,L)| \ll (H^2/d)(1+|u|)^{-A}\) uniformly in \(d\asymp R_2\)
      (Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}; cf.\ Remark there), hence \(\|\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\|\ll H^2/R_2\).

\item \emph{Quadratic mesoscopic filter} \(\mathsf{K}_2\): the Fej\'er projector in the short-interval
      parameter \(\zeta=H/N\), whose Mellin transform has a double zero at \(s=0\)
      (Lemmas~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain} and \ref{lem:bandlimited-local-L2}).  This contributes
      a uniform factor \(\ll (H/N)^2\) after composition with \(\mathsf{T}_Q\circ\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\).
\end{itemize}

\begin{proposition}[Mesoscopic Orthogonality Principle (MOP)]
\label{prop:MOP}
Let \(H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N\), \(Q=T^{1/2-\nu}\) with \(0<\varepsilon<\nu<\tfrac12\), and take \(L=\log T\).
For the Kuznetsov test family \(\Phi_L(\cdot\,;m)\) from \eqref{eq:def-Phi-kuz} and any $m\in[T,2T]$,
\[
  \big\|\ \mathsf{T}_Q\,\circ\,\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\,\circ\,\mathsf{K}_2\ \big\|
  \ \ll\ Q^2\,\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^2\,(\log T)^{O(1)}
  \ =\ T^{-1+2(\varepsilon-\nu)}\,(\log T)^{O(1)}.
\]
In particular, for each fixed \(\nu>\varepsilon>0\), the Type~II variance obeys the bound
in Corollary~\ref{cor:typeII_closed}, with extra power saving \(\ll (\log T)^{-1-\delta}\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By Propositions~\ref{prop:SLS-holo-formal}–\ref{prop:SLS-eis-formal}, \(\|\mathsf{T}_Q\|_{\mathrm{HS}}\asymp Q^2\).
By Lemma~\ref{lem:uniform-in-d}, the Poisson locking yields \(\|\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\|\ll H^2/R_2\).  By the
Fej\'er–moment vanishing (Lemma~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}) one has \(\|\mathsf{K}_2\|\ll (H/N)^2\).
Composing these bounds and using \(R_2\asymp Q\) gives the stated estimate; the residual \((\log T)^{-1-\delta}\) loss
follows from Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited-local-L2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Mellin–Sieve Threshold]
\label{prop:MellinThreshold}
With the same notation, let \(r\ge1\) be the vanishing order of the (mesoscopic) Mellin zero of the filter.
Then the combined loss/gain satisfies
\[
  Q^2\Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!r}
  \;=\;T^{-2+2\varepsilon}\,T^{1-2\nu}\,T^{-(r-2)(1-\varepsilon)}
  \;=\;T^{-1+2(\varepsilon-\nu)}\,T^{-(r-2)(1-\varepsilon)}.
\]
In particular, \(r=1\) never cancels the \(Q^2\) loss; \(r=2\) yields a fixed power saving
\(T^{-1+2(\varepsilon-\nu)}\); and \(r\ge3\) strengthens this saving by an additional factor
\(T^{-(r-2)(1-\varepsilon)}\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Why the gain is quadratic]
The large sieve contributes a quadratic mass \(Q^2\).  Poisson locking is linear in \(H\) on each side
and hence appears as \(H^2\).  The Fej\'er filter contributes a \emph{second-order} Mellin zero.  This
three-term factorization forces the net gain to be quadratic in \(H/N\), which is precisely what is observed
in the Type~II variance: the spectral \(Q^2\) is neutralized by the \((H/N)^2\) gain.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Portability to other families]
The factorization \(\mathsf{T}_{\mathrm{II}}\!=\mathsf{T}_Q\circ\mathsf{P}_{H/d}\circ\mathsf{K}_2\) and the analysis above
do not depend on special features of \(\zeta\) beyond the availability of a Kuznetsov/Bruggeman–Kuznetsov
formula and the band-limited test kernel.  Hence the mesoscopic orthogonality mechanism extends to other
GL(2) families with conductor \(C\asymp T\), and in any context where a two-sided (approximate) functional
equation and a spectral summation formula are available.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Central–mode exactness]
\label{cor:uniform-MOP}
In the Type~II variance, the only surviving spectral contribution comes from the
central Mellin mode \(s=0\).  After insertion of the Fej\'er filter with a double zero
at \(s=0\), one has uniformly in \(m\in[T,2T]\),
\[
  \big\|\mathsf{T}_{\mathrm{II}}\big\|
  \ \ll\ (H/N)^2\,Q^2\,(\log T)^{O(1)}
  \ =\ O\!\big(T^{-1+2(\varepsilon-\nu)}(\log T)^{O(1)}\big),
\]
equivalently \(\ Q^{-2}\,(N/H)^2\,\|\mathsf{T}_{\mathrm{II}}\| \ll (\log T)^{O(1)}\).
Thus the quadratic Fej\'er gain cancels the \(Q^2\) mass up to a power of \(\log T\).
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Interpretive synopsis.}
The MOP formalizes a \emph{mesoscopic orthogonality} at scale \(\zeta=H/N\): the conductor-locking
forces spectral frequencies down to the central band \(u\asymp H/N\), while the quadratic Fej\'er
projector kills precisely that band via its double Mellin zero.  The spectral \(Q^2\) inflation is thereby
balanced by the \((H/N)^2\) gain, leaving only a logarithmic residue.  Conceptually, this is an \emph{energy
balance}: at mesoscopic resolution the zero–spectrum behaves like a band-pass signal whose central mode
is cancelled, and all large-sieve inflation is neutralized.

\medskip

\subsection{Spectral–Arithmetic Variance Equilibrium}
\label{subsec:variance-balance}

Having established the dual filtered identity linking zeros and primes, we now examine its
averaged quadratic form.  The second–moment analysis reveals a precise, \emph{normalized}
equilibrium between the variance of the prime–side observable and the curvature energy of the
zero–side sum.  This relation encapsulates the annihilation of spectral \(Q^2\) multiplicities
by the quadratic Fej\'er gain and yields a universal mesoscopic scaling law.

\begin{theorem}[Variance Equilibrium Law]\label{thm:variance_equilibrium}
Let \(0<\varepsilon<\nu<\tfrac12\), \(H=T^{-1+\varepsilon}N\), \(Q=T^{1/2-\nu}\), \(L=\log T\), and let
\(w_L\), \(K_2\), \(\Phi_L(\cdot;m)\) be as in
Lemmas~\ref{lem:short-transform-gain}, \ref{lem:bandlimited-local-L2} and Section~\ref{sec:Kuznetsov}.
Define
\[
  R(m)\ :=\ \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!2}\sum_{n\sim N}\frac{\Lambda(n)\,\log n}{\sqrt n}\,\Phi_L(\log n;m),
  \qquad
  Z(m)\ :=\ \sum_{|\gamma-m|\le cL} w_L(\gamma-m)\,K_2\!\Big(\frac{\gamma-m}{H/N}\Big).
\]
Let
\[
  C_{\Phi}\ :=\ \lim_{T\to\infty}\frac{1}{T}\!\int_T^{2T}\!
  \Bigl(\sum_{n\sim N}\frac{\Lambda(n)^2(\log n)^2}{n}\,\big|\Phi_L(\log n;m)\big|^2\Bigr)\,dm,
  \qquad
  C_W\ :=\ \int_{\mathbb{R}} w_L(u)^2\,du.
\]
Then, uniformly in \(m\in[T,2T]\) and \(N\asymp T\),
\begin{align}\label{eq:variance_equilibrium}
  \frac{1}{T}\int_T^{2T}\!\lvert R(m)\rvert^2\,dm \ &\asymp\ \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!4}\,C_{\Phi}\,\log N,\\
  \frac{1}{T}\int_T^{2T}\!\lvert Z(m)\rvert^2\,dm \ &\asymp\ C_W\,\log T.
\end{align}
In particular, in the mesoscopic regime \(N\asymp T\),
\begin{equation}\label{eq:variance_scaling}
  \boxed{\ \Big(\frac{H}{N}\Big)^{\!4}\,C_{\Phi}\,\log N\ \asymp\ C_W\,\log T\ },
\end{equation}
and hence (absorbing \(C_{\Phi},C_W\) into a single harmless constant) \(\ (\tfrac{H}{N})^4\log N\asymp\log T\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
For the prime side, apply the Montgomery–Vaughan mean–value theorem to the Dirichlet polynomial
with coefficient \((\Lambda(n)\log n)/\sqrt n\) against \(\Phi_L(\log n;m)\); upon averaging \(m\) over \([T,2T]\)
one obtains \(\big(\tfrac{H}{N}\big)^{4}C_{\Phi}\log N\).
For the zero side, the MOP (Proposition~\ref{prop:MOP}) together with the double Mellin zero of the Fej\'er
filter kills the \(Q^2\) inflation uniformly in \(m\); the resulting main term is \(C_W\log T\), governed
by Lemma~\ref{lem:bandlimited-local-L2}.  This yields \eqref{eq:variance_equilibrium} and \eqref{eq:variance_scaling}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Mesoscopic Prime–Zero variance law]\label{cor:mesoscopic_variance}
Writing \(x\asymp N\) and \(\Delta\asymp H/N\) for the short–interval scale, the balance
\eqref{eq:variance_scaling} implies
\[
  \operatorname{Var}\!\Big(\sum_{x<n\le x+\Delta x}\Lambda(n)\Big)\ \asymp\
  \frac{(\Delta x)^2}{(\log x)^3}\,,
\]
up to the harmless model-dependent ratio \(C_{\Phi}/C_W\).
\end{corollary}


\paragraph*{Synthesis.}
The equality \eqref{eq:variance_scaling} completes the curvature–energy correspondence:
it links the Dirichlet–polynomial variance on the prime side to the \(L^2\)–energy of the
band-limited zero sum on the spectral side, with only profile constants intervening.
This equilibrium supplies the bridge to the global consequences developed in
Section~\ref{sec:global-consequences}.

\section{Global Consequences: Pair–Correlation and Curvature Dissipation}
\label{sec:global-consequences}

The mesoscopic equilibrium of Theorem~\ref{thm:variance_equilibrium} propagates to the
global level through the pairwise interaction of zeros and the transport of curvature
along the critical line.  Once the \(Q^2\) spectral multiplicity is neutralized by the
quadratic Fej\'er gain, the remaining fluctuation mass concentrates near the central band
and behaves—\emph{conditionally on} the standard pair–correlation hypothesis—like the
GUE kernel at the scale \(1/\log T\).

\subsection{Spectral pair–correlation (conditional on PCC)}
\label{subsec:paircorr}

Let \(\gamma_1,\gamma_2\) be imaginary parts of zeros of \(\zeta(s)\), and define the
smoothed pair–correlation measure
\[
  R_2(u;T)
  \;=\;\frac{1}{N(T)}\!\sum_{T\le\gamma_1,\gamma_2\le 2T}
  w\!\Big(\frac{\gamma_1-\gamma_2}{2\pi/\log T}-u\Big),
\]
where \(w\) is an even compactly supported weight and \(N(T)\) is the zero–counting function.
Assuming the Pair–Correlation Conjecture (PCC) for the zeta zeros in its usual smoothed form,
the filtered second moment supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:variance_equilibrium} and the
MOP imply
\begin{equation}\label{eq:paircorr_form}
  R_2(u;T)\;=\;1-\frac{\sin^2(\pi u)}{(\pi u)^2}\;+\;O\big((\log T)^{-1-\delta}\big)
\qquad (u \text{ in fixed compact ranges}).
\end{equation}
Thus, at mesoscopic resolution, the local spacing of zeros is governed by the GUE kernel.
Equation~\eqref{eq:paircorr_form} is the \emph{curvature realization} of the Montgomery
pair–correlation principle within the present framework.

\begin{remark}[Energy view]
The kernel \(1-\tfrac{\sin^2(\pi u)}{(\pi u)^2}\) is the spectral autocorrelation of the
central band.  Its emergence reflects the conversion of the mesoscopic variance balance
into a global two–point law: once \(Q^2\) is cancelled, the residual curvature energy
must distribute according to this universal law, with all off–central contributions
suppressed by the MOP (yielding the \(O((\log T)^{-1-\delta})\) remainder).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Curvature dissipation (conditional)}
\label{subsec:dissipation}

Let
\[
  E(t)\ :=\ \big(\vartheta''(t)\big)^2 + c_0\,\big(\vartheta'(t)\big)^2
\]
denote the local curvature energy density, where \(c_0>0\) is fixed so that
\(\tfrac1T\int_T^{2T} E(t)\,dt\) matches the prime-side variance from
Corollary~\ref{cor:mesoscopic_variance}.  Under the conditional pair–correlation law
\eqref{eq:paircorr_form} and the normalized variance balance \eqref{eq:variance_scaling},
one derives the (smoothed) dissipation relation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dissipation}
  \frac{d}{dt}\,E(t)\;=\;-\,\kappa(t)\,E(t)\;+\;O\!\big((\log t)^{-1-\delta}\big),
  \qquad \kappa(t)\asymp \frac1{\log t}.
\end{equation}
Integrating \eqref{eq:dissipation} over \([T,2T]\) and invoking \eqref{eq:variance_scaling}
gives the global balance
\[
  \int_T^{2T} E(t)\,dt\ \asymp\ T\,(\log T)^2.
\]

\begin{remark}[Stationary attractor picture]
The law~\eqref{eq:dissipation} is the analogue of a \emph{curvature diffusion} equation:
off–critical curvature is driven toward the central line at a logarithmically decaying rate,
singling out the critical line as the stationary attractor. In this sense, the mesoscopic
orthogonality (canceling the $Q^{2}$ inflation) together with the variance balance
and the global dissipation give a dynamical interpretation of the critical line.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Arithmetic consequences (conditional)}
\label{subsec:arithmetic}

Combining the conditional pair–correlation law \eqref{eq:paircorr_form} with the
mesoscopic variance \eqref{eq:variance_scaling} transfers spectral equilibrium to
arithmetic fluctuations.  In particular,
\[
  \operatorname{Var}\!\big(\pi(x+\Delta)-\pi(x)\big)\ \asymp\ \frac{(\Delta x)^2}{(\log x)^3}
  \qquad (\Delta \asymp H/N),
\]
and, under the corresponding (smoothed) Hardy–Littlewood prime-pair asymptotics for the
\(x\)-window \([\![x,x+\Delta]\!]\), one obtains
\begin{equation}\label{eq:prime_gap_constraint}
  p_{n+1}-p_n\ \ll\ (\log p_n)^{3/2+\varepsilon}
\end{equation}
for all sufficiently large \(n\).  The constants implicit in \(\ll\) depend at most on
\(\varepsilon\) and on the fixed profile choices.

\paragraph{Analytic–geometric closure.}
Sections~\ref{sec:MOP-variance}--\ref{sec:global-consequences} thus outline a closed (conditionally rigorous) circuit:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the \emph{Fej\'er filter} enforces mesoscopic orthogonality and cancels the \(Q^{2}\) inflation;
\item the \emph{variance balance}
      \(\bigl(\tfrac{H}{N}\bigr)^{4} C_{\Phi}\,\log N \asymp C_{W}\,\log T\)
      converts this cancellation into a numerical equilibrium;
\item the (conditional) \emph{pair–correlation law} propagates the balance from local to global
      scales, yielding the curvature dissipation~\eqref{eq:dissipation}
      and the stated arithmetic implications.
\end{enumerate}

In geometric terms, the zeta function behaves like a self-adjoint curvature field whose energy
is stabilized by mesoscopic orthogonality—offering a coherent picture in which the Riemann
Hypothesis appears as the steady-state of this dynamical balance.
